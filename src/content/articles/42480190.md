### 重點摘要
這項質性研究旨在探討晚期癌症患者和醫護人員對於鴉片類藥物引起的便秘（OIC）預防的經驗與看法，並評估其在教育和溝通方面的認知。研究透過深度訪談11位癌症患者和27位醫護人員，並進行主題分析，辨識出病人教育、知識水平、便秘討論及醫護人員信念四個主題。結果顯示，患者常不記得醫護人員提供的資訊，缺乏明確的指導，且對腸道功能的了解程度高於醫護人員預期。本研究結論指出，在OIC預防方面，病人教育和醫護-病人溝通存在明顯落差，未來應著重於利用教育工具、結構性溝通及監測排便狀況。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
本研究透過探索患者與醫護人員在鴉片類藥物引起的便秘預防、教育和溝通上的經驗與認知，揭示了現有的資訊傳遞和理解上的實踐差距。未來的研究應著重於開發和評估有效的教育工具，建立結構化的溝通方式，並加強對患者排便狀況的監測，以改善現有預防措施的不足。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Experiences of patients with cancer and healthcare professionals with opioid-induced constipation: A qualitative study.
**Abstract:** Opioids are frequently prescribed for cancer-related pain, with opioid-induced constipation (OIC) being the most commonly reported side-effect. OIC in patients with cancer can be difficult to treat, underlining the importance of adequate prevention. The primary aim of this study was to explore the experiences and beliefs of patients with advanced cancer, and of healthcare professionals (HCPs) regarding the prevention of OIC. As a secondary aim, we investigated patients' and HCPs' experiences and perceptions of education and communication around prevention of OIC. A qualitative, explorative study using semi-structured, in-depth interviews with both patients and HCPs (physicians, nurse practitioners, nurses and pharmacy assistants). Data were analyzed using thematic analysis. Eleven patients with cancer and twenty-seven HCPs were recruited from a single, academic hospital in Amsterdam. The mean duration of the interviews was 22 min (range 12-32 min) among patients and 15 min (range 9-29 min) among HCPs. Four main themes were identified: (1) Patient education on prevention of OIC; (2) Levels of knowledge of patients and HCPs; (3) Discussing constipation; (4) Beliefs of HCPs regarding prevention. Patients often do not recall the information professionals believe they have provided, prefer to receive information from their physician, demonstrate more knowledge about bowel function than HCPs assume, and lack clear guidance on when to contact their physician or how to manage their laxative use. Our findings reveal gaps in patient education and HCP-patient communication on the prevention of OIC. Future research should focus on the use of educational tools, structured communication and monitoring of bowel movements. There is a need for improved explanation about opioid-related side-effects through clear and repeated informing to the patients. Educational tools may support clear and consistent communication in helping patients better understand these side effects.
