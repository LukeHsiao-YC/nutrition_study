### 重點摘要
本研究旨在探討訊息格式與個人情境如何影響吸菸者尋求戒菸支持的意願，以克服未經協助戒菸的高復發率。研究招募386名吸菸者，將其隨機分配至非敘事、第一人稱敘事、聊天機器人敘事或控制組，並評估他們向人際或數位資源揭露經驗與尋求支持的意圖。結果顯示，聊天機器人遞送的敘事訊息顯著增加了向AI程式揭露經驗和尋求支持的意圖，尤其對於社會支持較低或近期無戒菸嘗試的參與者效果更佳。本研究結論指出，醫療專業人員可依據個案的社會支持狀況和戒菸階段調整訊息策略，整合敘事型聊天機器人可為有限資源的個體提供可及的戒菸支持途徑。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這項研究填補了關於聊天機器人遞送的敘事訊息如何影響吸菸者早期自我揭露與尋求戒菸支持意願的研究空白，特別是考量了社會支持程度與近期戒菸經驗等個人情境因素。它揭示了針對特定高危險群體（如缺乏社會支持或缺乏戒菸經驗者）進行訊息客製化的重要性，為未來開發更精準的數位戒菸介入策略提供了依據。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** From isolation to engagement: Chatbot-delivered narratives as a social surrogate for novice quitters and socially isolated smokers.
**Abstract:** Most smokers desire to quit, yet many rely on unassisted attempts that frequently result in relapses. Facilitating early self-disclosure and support-seeking is a critical first step toward effective cessation. This study examines how message format and a recipient's individual context impact their readiness to engage with various support sources. In an experiment, adult smokers (N = 386) were assigned to a control or one of three message conditions: non-narrative, first-person narrative, or chatbot-delivered narrative. The study evaluated intentions to disclose smoking-related experiences and seek support across interpersonal and digital sources, testing moderation by perceived social support and recent quit attempts. Chatbot-delivered narratives significantly increased intentions to disclose experiences to, and seek support from, AI-powered programs. Message engagement with interpersonal and group-based sources was contingent upon perceived social support and recent quit experience. Chatbot delivery increased AI-oriented sharing intention when friend support was low and increased friend sharing when support from significant others was low. For participants without recent quit attempts, narrative-based messages, particularly in chatbot format, were associated with increased intentions to disclose experiences and seek support through group-based services. It is important to align message strategies with individuals' social ties and stages of change. Narrative messages, particularly in chatbot format, may directly encourage AI-oriented engagement while also benefiting individuals with limited peer support or limited recent quitting experience. Health providers can screen perceived social support and recent quit attempts and tailor message delivery accordingly. Integrating narratives into counseling or program intake may encourage disclosure and support-seeking for those who have limited interpersonal and quitting-related resources. Narrative-based chatbots may complement existing services by offering accessible pathways into AI-powered and traditional cessation support.
