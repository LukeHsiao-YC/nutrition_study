### 重點摘要
本研究於高收入國家的海外屬地，針對面臨高生育率、文化多樣性與糧食不安全問題的孕婦，實施一項團體式營養介入計畫，旨在評估其執行忠誠度。研究採用混合方法設計，收集了受試者、執行者及利害關係人的定量與定性數據，以了解介入的實際運作情況。結果顯示，此介入計畫的實施大致符合原定方案，參與率達77%，且弱勢婦女的邊際排除有限，社會孤立感高的女性反而展現更高的參與承諾。儘管在介入清晰度、組織流程與中介角色方面存在改進空間，該介入方案仍被認為高度適當並受到各方好評，為後續的影響評估提供了重要基礎。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這項研究填補了在特定高收入國家海外屬地中，針對孕婦的團體營養介入計畫「實施忠誠度」的評估空白，而非直接評估其健康成果。它深入探討了介入的實際執行情況、參與者反應及潛在的改進機會，為後續的影響評估提供了關鍵的基礎資訊，並有助於理解類似介入模式在不同情境下的可轉移性。然而，本研究並未評估介入對孕婦營養狀況或糧食不安全的具體影響，這將是未來研究的重點。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Implementation fidelity of a pilot group-based nutrition intervention for pregnant women (Nutri Pou Ti' Moun 2): a mixed methods assessment.
**Abstract:** In the context of an overseas territory of a high-income country characterized by high fertility rates, cultural diversity, and food insecurity, a small-scale collective intervention with a focus on nutrition was implemented among pregnant women. Inspired by centering-based group care, without the health care component and with a food aid component, the interactive group sessions were co-facilitated by an expert on the session's topic and a peer-facilitator. The objective of the study was to assess the implementation fidelity to determine whether upcoming intervention outcomes are driven by the innovation or its implementation, and identify opportunities for improvement. The study employed a mixed-methods design and was guided by the Conceptual Framework for Implementation Fidelity. Quantitative data regarding the intervention recipients were collected through questionnaires, intervention worksheets and medical records. Semi-structured interviews were used to collect qualitative information about the experience of the intervention recipients. Data among deliverers and other stakeholders were collected quantitatively through self-administered questionnaires and qualitatively through semi-structured interviews. Descriptive, bivariate, and multivariate analyses were performed using Stata 19.5 software to determine the sociodemographic characteristics of intervention recipients and the determinants of relative assiduity. Overall, implementation was aligned with the original plan, with only a few minor adaptations. Despite contextual barriers and competing survival priorities, 77% of the 122 intervention recipients attended at least half of the sessions, indicating a satisfactory participation rate. The profile of recipients showed that marginal exclusion of the most socioeconomically disadvantaged women appears to have been limited, and bivariate analysis revealed that women who reported suffering from social isolation actually showed greater commitment. Conversely, educational attainment was associated with higher assiduity in nutrition-focused sessions, as opposed to living in a single-adult household. Although the intervention demonstrated high appropriateness and was well-received from recipients and all stakeholders, opportunities for improvement were identified regarding intervention clarity, organizational processes, and the scope of the mediation role. These findings inform the subsequent impact evaluation and its mechanisms of action. They also shed light on the potential transferability of a group-based intervention that addresses determinants of food insecurity during the first 1,000 days. Clinicaltrials.gov number: NCT06528535.
