### 重點摘要
本系統性回顧旨在探討能力、機會與動機（COM-B 模型）如何影響青少年永續且健康的飲食行為，並識別關鍵障礙與促進因素，以應對當前食物系統對環境與健康的雙重壓力。研究檢索了多個醫學資料庫，納入25項探討18歲以下學齡個體飲食行為的研究，發現動機、機會和能力被頻繁檢視，其中缺乏知識是普遍障礙，但單獨作用有限。味道是影響青少年選擇永續健康飲食的最強障礙，他們偏好肉類及熟悉食物，而味道也受熟悉度、經驗、食物認同和社會規範等環境及動機因素影響。總結而言，青少年永續健康飲食行為受多種交互作用因素形塑，未來介入應多面向切入，特別是針對味道和社會影響。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
本研究透過系統性回顧，填補了在青少年永續與健康飲食行為領域中，運用COM-B行為改變模型，系統性地分析各能力、機會、動機因素及其交互作用的知識空白，特別強調了味道與社會影響作為關鍵障礙的深層機制，並明確指出未來介入措施需採取多面向策略而非單一因素。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Capabilities, Opportunities, and Motivations Influencing Young People's Sustainable and Healthy Food-Related Behaviours: A Systematic Review.
**Abstract:** The present-day food system places a heavy burden on environmental sustainability and human health, underscoring the need for sustainable and healthy food behaviours. Engaging youth in this transition is vital, as dietary habits formed early in life have long-lasting impacts. This systematic review synthesises evidence on how capabilities, opportunities, and motivations-derived from the Capability, Opportunity, Motivation-Behaviour (COM-B) model-and their interactions relate to sustainable and healthy food behaviours among youth, identifying key barriers and facilitators. Medline, Embase, PsycInfo, and Web of Science were searched for studies exploring at least two COM-B components associated with sustainable and healthy food behaviours in school-aged individuals aged 18 years and younger. Studies addressed plant-forward diets, local or seasonal foods, food waste, or general sustainable food practices. Factors were classified as barriers or facilitators using the COM-B framework. Twenty-five studies met the inclusion criteria. Motivation (n = 22), opportunity (n = 22), and capability (n = 19) were examined with comparable frequency. Lack of knowledge was the most studied barrier, but knowledge alone rarely changed behaviour. Taste was the strongest barrier, with young people preferring meat and familiar foods over seasonal or local options. Studies examining interrelations between COM-B components (n = 11) showed that taste was shaped by environmental and motivational factors, including familiarity, prior experience, food identity, and social norms. Sustainable and healthy eating in young people is shaped by interacting capability, opportunity, and motivation factors. Interventions are likely most effective when targeting multiple COM-B domains, particularly by addressing taste and social influences.
