### 重點摘要
這項研究旨在評估過量體脂肪對韓國癌症負擔的影響，並比較身體質量指數 (BMI) 和腰圍 (WC) 作為衡量標準的歸因分數。研究使用韓國國家健康保險服務的440多萬成人數據，分析了從2012年篩檢至2020年期間的癌症病例。結果顯示，過量體脂肪在韓國導致了顯著的癌症負擔，例如2022年估計有超過22,000例癌症可歸因於過量體脂肪（依腰圍衡量）。研究強調腰圍能更有效地捕捉內臟脂肪，且單獨使用BMI可能會低估癌症負擔，因此建議加強腹部肥胖管理以預防亞洲人群的癌症。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這項研究填補了亞洲地區過量體脂肪對癌症影響評估不足的空白。它也指出單獨使用身體質量指數 (BMI) 可能會大幅低估癌症負擔，因為腰圍 (WC) 在捕捉內臟脂肪方面更有效。未來的研究可能需要更深入地探討吸菸對過量體脂肪與癌症風險關聯的潛在遮蔽效應。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Fractions and number of cancer cases attributable to excess body fat in South Korea.
**Abstract:** Excess body fat (EBF) is a major cancer risk factor, yet its impact in Asians remains underexplored. We estimated the population-attributable fraction (PAF) of EBF for various cancers in Korea, comparing estimates based on body mass index (BMI) and waist circumference (WC). Relative risks were derived from Korean National Health Insurance Service data of 4 441 103 adults screened in 2012, with 242 243 cancer cases identified through 2020. PAFs were calculated by using 2022 national obesity prevalence and cancer registry data. Sensitivity analyses were conducted among never smokers. The PAFs for 13 established obesity-associated cancers reached 5.48% (BMI) and 7.65% (WC) in men, 5.48% (BMI) and 6.68% (WC) in women aged <50 years, and 6.86% (BMI) and 9.34% (WC) in women aged ≥50 years. This translates into 16 534.2 avoidable-cancer cases in 2022 via BMI and 22 486.9 via WC. In sensitivity analyses, positive associations between obesity and cancer risk were more pronounced among never smokers, suggesting that smoking may mask the true impact of EBF in the general population. EBF significantly contributes to the cancer burden in Korea. Using BMI as the sole measure may substantially underestimate this burden compared with WC, which captures visceral adiposity more effectively. Our findings emphasize the importance of abdominal obesity management and support evidence-based public health policies for cancer prevention in Asian populations.
