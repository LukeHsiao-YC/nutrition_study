### 重點摘要
本研究旨在探討在聯邦X豁免權取消後，美國各州法規是否仍保留X豁免權元素，對丁丙諾啡處方醫生比率的影響。研究採用2021年至2023年的數據，使用比較性中斷時間序列模型分析每月處方醫生比率。結果顯示，州法規是否提及X豁免權，對於聯邦X豁免權取消後丁丙諾啡處方醫生的水準或趨勢均無顯著影響。研究結論認為，單純移除州級法定的X豁免權參考條款，可能無法擴大丁丙諾啡的可近性，因為結構性障礙依然存在。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這項研究填補了在聯邦X豁免權廢除後，州級法規對丁丙諾啡處方醫生比率具體影響的知識空白。其研究限制在於，雖然指出移除法規文字不足以擴大可近性，但並未深入探究或識別那些持續存在的「結構性障礙」為何，這為未來的研究指明了方向。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** State X-waiver Statutes and Buprenorphine Prescriber Rates Following Federal X-waiver Elimination, 2021-2023.
**Abstract:** This study examined whether U.S. jurisdictions that retained elements of the X-waiver in their statutes after federal elimination had lower buprenorphine prescriber rates than jurisdictions that did not retain such elements. Using a 25% random sample from IQVIA PharMetrics® Plus for Academics, data from January 2021-October 2023 (analyzed from 2025-2026) were modeled using Comparative Interrupted Time Series (CITS) models of monthly rates of unique buprenorphine prescribers per 10,000 eligible prescribers and jurisdiction fixed-effects generalized estimating equations (GEE). Jurisdictions were classified by whether they incorporated X-waiver language into their statutes, at the jurisdiction-month level, per the Center for Public Health Law Research at Temple University. Models were adjusted for three-month rolling opioid use disorder (OUD) diagnosis rates and annual age-adjusted opioid overdose mortality rates. Analyses were stratified by provider type. Three sensitivity analyses tested an alternative specification and excluded jurisdictions with unstable buprenorphine prescribing rates and enrollment. Forty-nine jurisdictions and 1,558 jurisdiction-months were included. Mean buprenorphine prescriber rates were higher in jurisdictions with X-waiver references than without (96.84 vs 47.01 buprenorphine prescribers/10,000 eligible prescribers). No differences in either level (-8.69 per 10,000 [95% CI: -19.96 to 2.59; p=0.131]) or trend (+0.74 per month [95% CI: -0.92 to 2.40; p=0.380]) of buprenorphine prescribers were found between jurisdiction-months with and without references to the X-waiver following X-waiver elimination. Findings were consistent across strata and sensitivity analyses. Findings suggest that removing jurisdiction-level statutory X-waiver references is unlikely to expand access, as structural barriers persist.
