### 重點摘要
第二型糖尿病患者常因病情和自我管理目標未達成而自責，本研究旨在探討正向與負向自我同情、情緒平衡、自我照護效能和創傷後成長與香港第二型糖尿病患者心理困擾之間的關聯。研究對416名患者進行橫斷面調查，結果顯示負向自我同情與心理困擾顯著相關，且情緒平衡與自我照護效能中介了自我同情與心理困擾的關係，但創傷後成長則無此作用。研究結論指出，負向自我同情對心理困擾影響較大，建議透過減少負向自我同情、提升自我照護技能及促進正向情緒的介入措施，改善患者的心理困擾。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這項研究填補了過去文獻中，對於正向與負向自我同情在第二型糖尿病患者心理調適中各自貢獻的空白，並釐清了情緒平衡、自我照護效能以及創傷後成長如何中介自我同情與心理困擾之間的關係。然而，本研究採用橫斷面設計，未能確立變數間的因果關係。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** The roles of diabetes-specific self-compassion, affect balance, and self-care self-efficacy in psychological distress among Hong Kong patients with Type 2 diabetes.
**Abstract:** Patients with Type 2 diabetes (T2D) often blame themselves for their conditions and not reaching their self-management goals. Guided by the self-regulation resource model, self-compassion facilitates adjustments to chronic diseases through increasing affective and cognitive resources like positive affect and self-care self-efficacy. However, the contributions of positive and negative self-compassion (PSC and NSC) in psychological adjustment among patients with T2D have not been examined. Studies also suggest that patients with T2D experience positive changes from their illness journey (i.e., posttraumatic growth), but whether posttraumatic growth explains the link between self-compassion and psychological adjustment has not been understood. This study examined the associations between PSC / NSC and psychological distress among Hong Kong patients with T2D and explore whether affect balance (i.e., how positive affect outweighed negative affect), self-care self-efficacy, and posttraumatic growth mediated such associations. A sample of 416 patients with T2D recruited from public hospitals in Hong Kong completed a cross-sectional survey measuring the aforementioned variables. Multiple mediation analyses were conducted using SPSS Process Macro Model 4. From correlation analysis, NSC, but not PSC, was associated with psychological distress. Mediation model results indicated that the relationships between PSC / NSC and psychological distress were significantly mediated by affect balance and self-care self-efficacy. However, posttraumatic growth did not mediate the relationship between self-compassion and psychological distress. The direct effect from NSC, but not PSC, on psychological distress remained significant after accounting for the mediators. NSC contributed more than PSC to psychological distress among Hong Kong patients with T2D. Self-care self-efficacy and affect balance, but not posttraumatic growth, significantly mediated the associations between both aspects of self-compassion and psychological distress. Interventions guiding patients to reduce NSC, develop skills for self-care activities, and facilitate positive affect might improve psychological distress among Hong Kong patients with T2D.
