### 重點摘要
鑑於新生兒照護中存在顯著的種族與族裔不平等，且準確的背景資料是推動健康公平品質改善的關鍵，本研究旨在解決新生兒無法自我報告造成的資料收集困難。研究團隊在一個大型學術三級新生兒加護病房（NICU）實施了一項品質改善計畫，透過病人註冊流程優化和跨部門合作（如與出生登記處、婦產科協作），旨在提高種族與族裔人口統計資訊的完整性與準確性。在前置評估期，NICU患者中約有30%的種族與族裔資料缺失或不明，但經過三次「計畫-執行-學習-行動」（PDSA）循環後，此比例成功降至10%。本研究透過多部門合作，顯著改善了NICU中人口統計資料的準確性，為未來實施以健康公平為重點的品質改善奠定了基礎。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這項研究成功證明了透過多部門合作可以顯著提高新生兒加護病房中種族與族裔人口統計資料的完整性和準確性，填補了在無法自我報告的脆弱族群中有效收集此類敏感人口資料的方法學空白。然而，文獻中明確指出，此舉僅是「實施以公平為重點的品質改善的第一步」，因此其主要限制在於並未直接實施或評估基於這些改進數據的健康公平介入措施，而僅止於資料收集層面的改善。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Improving Race and Ethnicity Data Collection in an Academic Neonatal Intensive Care Unit.
**Abstract:** There are significant racial and ethnic inequities in neonatal care and outcomes. Accurate race and ethnicity demographic information is the cornerstone of equity-focused quality improvement. The collection of accurate race and ethnicity demographic information is complex for neonates that cannot self-report and do not yet have personal identity formation. In a large, academic, level III neonatal intensive care unit (NICU), we implemented a quality improvement initiative aimed at increasing the completeness and accuracy of race and ethnicity demographic information in patient records. We conducted a quality improvement initiative using data from January 2021 to June 2025 among patients admitted to a large, academic, level III NICU. The primary goal was to reduce the percentage of missing or unknown racial and ethnic demographic information. Our study included a preintervention data quality assessment period followed by 3 plan-do-study-act (PDSA) cycles focused on patient registration and cross-departmental collaborations with the birth registry and the Department of Obstetrics and Gynecology. During the preintervention data quality assessment, we demonstrated a steady state with 30% of patients admitted to the NICU having missing or unknown race and ethnicity demographic information. We observed 3 shifts associated with PDSA cycles. During the last PDSA cycle, the missing or unknown demographic information was 10%. We decreased the proportion of missing or unknown demographic data in a large, academic, level III NICU through a multidisciplinary hospital-wide collaboration. This initiative serves as the first step to implement equity-focused quality improvement.
