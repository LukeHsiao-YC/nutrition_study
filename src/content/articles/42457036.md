### 重點摘要
本研究旨在探討早期懷孕階段的母體代謝體特徵與兒童出生至兩歲生長軌跡之間的關聯。研究分析了1,152對母子在孕期10-13週的母體空腹血清代謝體，並追蹤兒童體重身長Z分數的變化來定義生長加速或減速。結果顯示，早期懷孕的多元不飽和縮醛磷脂膽鹼與生長加速相關，而不飽和脂肪酸和支鏈胺基酸則與生長減速有關，早期懷孕脂質整體與生命早期生長加速呈現正相關。這些發現為早期懷孕的代謝體變化如何塑造兒童發育提供了新的見解。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
本研究填補了先前文獻中對於早期懷孕母體代謝體特徵如何影響兒童生命早期生長軌跡探討不足的研究空白，證明了在孕期早期檢測到的代謝體組，特別是脂質，與兒童的生長模式有顯著關聯。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Early Pregnancy Metabolomic Signatures for Child Growth Trajectories: A Prospective Metabolome-wide Association Study.
**Abstract:** Suboptimal early-life growth trajectories are predictors of later cardiometabolic risk. While mid- to late-pregnancy maternal metabolites have been linked to fetal and birth outcomes, the role of early pregnancy metabolomic profiles in child growth trajectories are underexplored. We examined associations between early pregnancy metabolites and child growth acceleration and deceleration from birth to age one and two years. Among 1,152 mother-child dyads from the prospective Pregnancy Environment and Lifestyle Study, maternal untargeted metabolomics were measured by gas chromatography/time-of-flight mass spectrometry, reversed phase-, and hydrophilic interaction liquid chromatography/quadrupole time-of-flight mass spectrometry using fasting serum collected at gestational weeks 10-13. Of this sample, 813 had child anthropometric data at age one and 770 at age two years. Growth trajectories were defined by changes in weight-for-length z-scores from birth to age one or two years: growth acceleration (≥0.67 standard deviations (SD)), growth deceleration (≤-0.67 SD), extreme growth acceleration (≥1.34 SD), and extreme growth deceleration (≤-1.34 SD), with normal growth (-0.67-0.67 SD) as reference. Poisson regression examined associations between metabolites and child growth trajectories. Chemical enrichment analysis identified metabolite clusters associated with child growth trajectories, adjusted for false discovery rate (FDR). A total of 431 annotated metabolites were identified. From birth to age one year, the polyunsaturated plasmalogen phosphatidylcholine cluster was associated with growth acceleration, while unsaturated fatty acids and polyunsaturated fatty acids were associated with growth deceleration (all FDR-corrected p <0.05). Polyunsaturated plasmalogen phosphatidylcholine and unsaturated phosphatidylcholine clusters were associated with extreme growth acceleration from birth to one year, dicarboxylic acids with extreme growth acceleration from birth to two years, and branched-chain amino acids with extreme growth deceleration from birth to two years (all FDR-corrected p <0.05). Early pregnancy lipids were positively associated with early-life growth acceleration and extreme growth acceleration. Findings may provide insights into how early pregnancy metabolomic changes shape development.
