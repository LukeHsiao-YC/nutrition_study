### 重點摘要
本試點隨機對照試驗旨在評估兩種蔬果處方配送機制（居家配送或憑證）對超重或肥胖且面臨營養不安全成人之可行性。研究招募了48名參與者進行8週干預，結果顯示高參與者保留率、處方兌換率和滿意度。參與者在營養安全、飲食品質、水果攝取、自我效能及收縮壓等方面均有顯著改善。兩種配送方式皆具可行性並能改善關鍵結果，但仍需更大規模研究以確立其成效。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這項試點研究證明了兩種蔬果處方配送機制在超重/肥胖且營養不安全成人中的可行性，並初步顯示對多項健康指標的益處，為弱勢族群的營養介入策略提供了實證依據。然而，由於這是初步研究且樣本量有限，未來需要更大規模的介入研究來確切評估這些方案的長期效果與成本效益。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Feasibility of Home-Delivery and Voucher-Based Produce Prescription Programs Among Adults with Overweight or Obesity and Nutrition Insecurity.
**Abstract:** Evaluate the feasibility of 2 produce prescription delivery mechanisms in a two-arm, pilot, randomized controlled trial at a Baltimore community market. Forty-eight adults with overweight or obesity experiencing nutrition insecurity (65% women, 83% non-Hispanic Black). Produce vouchers or home-delivered produce boxes for 8-weeks, with behavioral support. Recruitment, retention, fidelity, satisfaction, and changes in nutrition security, diet quality, self-efficacy, weight, and blood pressure. The Wilcoxon signed-rank test assessed changes in outcomes. Effect sizes were evaluated using the rank-biserial correlation and Kendall's W. Participant retention (88%), produce prescription redemption (75% to 99%), and satisfaction (93%) were high. Participant engagement in behavioral support varied. Participants had improvements in nutrition security (+0.5, r = 0.87), diet (+3.0, r = 0.50), fruit intake (+0.50, r = 0.64), self-efficacy (+3.0, r = 0.57), and systolic blood pressure (-4.0 mm Hg, r = -0.77). Both delivery mechanisms were feasible and led to improvements in key outcomes. Larger studies are needed to establish effectiveness.
