### 重點摘要
儘管飲食在預防和管理慢性病中扮演核心角色，目前許多健康專業培訓計畫中的營養教育仍顯不足。現代營養科學認識到飲食行為和健康結果受生物、行為、環境和食物系統等多重因素的複雜交互作用影響。本文提出一個跨學科的營養教育框架，整合了醫師、營養師、廚師和農民的專業知識。這種方法旨在連結臨床照護、營養科學、烹飪實踐和農業系統，以期加強證據轉化為實踐，提升健康專業人員的營養能力，並最終改善人群健康結果。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇文獻指出目前健康專業培訓課程中營養教育不足的現況，並提出一個整合醫師、營養師、廚師和農民的跨學科營養教育框架作為解決方案。它填補了如何將多面向的營養科學知識有效轉化為實踐的教育方法空白，並強調了未來需要將此概念性框架付諸實踐並評估其成效。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Nourishing Collaboration: Interdisciplinary Nutrition Education for Health Care Professionals.
**Abstract:** Nutrition education remains insufficient in many health professional training programs despite the central role of diet in the prevention and management of chronic disease. Contemporary nutrition science increasingly recognizes that dietary behaviors and health outcomes are shaped by complex interactions among biological, behavioral, environmental, and food system factors. This perspective proposes an interdisciplinary framework for nutrition education that integrates the complementary expertise of physicians, dietitians, chefs, and farmers. By bridging clinical care, nutrition science, culinary practice, and agricultural systems, such an approach may strengthen the translation of evidence into practice, improve nutrition-related competencies among health professionals, and ultimately enhance population health outcomes.
