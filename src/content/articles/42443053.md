### 重點摘要
該研究旨在探討主動通勤（步行或騎自行車）與被動通勤（乘車）上學的兒童在特定異位脂肪儲存、心血管代謝風險因子及胰島素阻抗方面的差異。研究分析了174名兒童的橫斷面基線數據，使用磁振造影（MRI）評估了多種異位脂肪，並測量了血壓、血脂、血糖和胰島素等心血管代謝指標。結果顯示，在異位脂肪儲存、心血管代謝風險因子或胰島素阻抗方面，主動通勤兒童與被動通勤兒童之間沒有觀察到顯著差異。因此，研究結論認為主動通勤上學與兒童的異位脂肪儲存、心血管代謝風險因子或胰島素阻抗無關。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這項研究釐清了主動通勤上學與兒童特定異位脂肪儲存及心血管代謝風險因子之間缺乏顯著關聯的議題，這在先前的研究中並不清楚。然而，作為一項橫斷面研究，它無法建立因果關係，也未能評估長期或累積效應，未來研究可考慮採用縱向設計進一步探討。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Differences in Fat Depots, Cardiometabolic Risk Factors, and Insulin Resistance Between Active and Passive School Commuters.
**Abstract:** Active commuting to and from school (ACS) is a feasible strategy to increase daily physical activity in children. Although ACS has been associated with favourable body composition and selected cardiometabolic markers, its association with specific ectopic fat depots remains unclear in paediatric populations. To examine differences in specific fat depots and cardiometabolic risk factors between children who actively and passively commute to school. Cross-sectional baseline data from 174 children (48.9% girls; mean age 10.8 ± 1.4 years) participating in the EFIGRO and MICROKID projects were analysed. ACS mode was self-reported using a validated questionnaire and classified as active or passive. Visceral adipose tissue, abdominal subcutaneous adipose tissue, intermuscular abdominal adipose tissue, hepatic fat, intrapancreatic fat, and lumbar spine marrow fat were assessed using magnetic resonance imaging. Cardiometabolic risk factors included blood pressure, triglycerides, high-density lipoprotein cholesterol, glucose, insulin, and insulin resistance estimated by the homeostasis model assessment. No significant differences were observed between active and passive commuters for any fat depot (all p ≥ 0.150) or cardiometabolic risk factors, including insulin resistance and the overall cardiometabolic risk score (all p ≥ 0.220). Active commuting to/from school was not associated with differences in ectopic fat depots, cardiometabolic risk factors, or insulin resistance in children.
