### 重點摘要
本研究旨在系統性回顧並量化地中海飲食（MD）依從性與6-19歲學齡兒童認知能力及學業表現之間的關聯。透過統合分析39項文獻，共26,307名參與者，結果顯示MD依從性與兒童整體認知能力呈現小但顯著的正相關（0.18；調整後0.12）。子群分析亦指出MD與學業成就、認知能力以及學習動機與策略均有小但顯著的正向關聯。研究結論支持推廣MD作為全面性支持兒童認知健康與學業成功的策略之一。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇文獻填補了學齡兒童地中海飲食依從性與認知能力及學業表現之間關聯缺乏全面性、量化證據的空白。儘管研究量化了此關聯，但納入研究間的高度異質性（如I2值）表明未來研究需更深入探討這些變異來源，並進一步驗證其因果關係。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Mediterranean Diet, Cognition, and Academic Performance in School-Aged Children: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis.
**Abstract:** Cognitive abilities in children, often reflected in their academic performance, predict educational and occupational performance, socioeconomic attainment, health, and longevity. Identification of modifiable factors affecting these abilities can influence multiple spheres across their lifespan. The aim of this review and meta-analysis was to systematically summarize and quantify the existing empirical evidence on associations between adherence to the Mediterranean diet (MD) and cognition outcomes and academic performance in school-aged children (aged 6-19 years). PubMed, Scopus, and Web of Science electronic databases were systematically searched in April 2025 for studies, examining cognitive effects of adherence to the MD in childhood. Study screening and risk-of-bias assessment were conducted independently by the authors. Multilevel meta-analyses accounted for dependent observations: individual effect sizes (level 1), nested within studies (level 2), and further comprised various cognition domains (level 3). Of 1140 articles initially identified, 39 studies with a total of 26 307 participants, were reviewed and analyzed. From these studies, we found a positive, small but significant correlation of 0.18 (95% CI, 0.10-0.26) between adherence to the MD and overall cognition in school-aged children. The findings persisted in the meta-analysis of studies with adjusted coefficients (0.12; 95% CI, 0.08-0.17). In subgroup analyses, correlations between adherence to the MD and specific facets of cognition were also small but significant for academic achievement: 0.21 [95% CI, 0.09-0.32], I2 = 82%, for cognitive abilities: 0.12 [95% CI, 0.04-0.19], I2 =63%, and for motivation and learning strategies, 0.35 [95% CI, 0.14-0.52], I2 = 99%. Adherence to the MD has a small but significant positive correlation with multiple cognition facets and academic performance in children aged 6-19 years. Promoting the MD may be considered as part of a broader approach to supporting cognitive health and academic success. PROSPERO registration No. [CRD420251049164].
