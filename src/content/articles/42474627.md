### 重點摘要
這項橫斷面研究旨在探討吸菸風險感知、母胎依附和感知壓力對懷孕期間吸菸狀況的預測能力。研究從伊利諾州兩間診所招募了懷孕24週以上且年滿18歲的女性，透過問卷收集數據。結果顯示，對吸菸風險感知越高，孕婦越不可能在懷孕期間吸菸，但母胎依附和感知壓力並非吸菸狀態的預測因子。本研究強調個人吸菸風險感知在改善戒菸介入和提升母嬰健康方面的重要性。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這項研究透過實證數據指出，在懷孕期間，孕婦對於吸菸風險的個人感知是預測其吸菸狀態的重要因子，而母胎依附和感知壓力則不然。這填補了對於影響孕婦懷孕期間吸菸行為之關鍵心理社會因素的具體理解空白，為未來制定更具針對性的戒菸介入措施提供了明確方向。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Perceived Tobacco Smoking Risk and Predictors of Smoking During Pregnancy: A Cross-Sectional Predictive Correlational Study.
**Abstract:** To examine the strength of perception of tobacco smoking risk, maternal-fetal attachment, and perceived stress as factors for predicting pregnant women's smoking status, after controlling for socioeconomic variables. This is a cross-sectional, predictive correlational study with a convenience sample recruited from a private prenatal clinic and a federally funded community health clinic in one central Illinois county. Women 18 years or older, at 24 weeks gestation or greater, were surveyed during their prenatal appointments, utilizing the Maternal Antenatal Attachment Scale, the Perceived Stress Scale, the Modified Perception of Pregnancy Risk Questionnaire (MPPRQ), and obstetric, demographic and socioeconomic questions to determine if maternal-fetal attachment, perceived stress, and perceived risks from smoking tobacco (smoking) predict smoking status during pregnancy. We found higher perceived smoking tobacco risk predicted women were likely to not currently use tobacco [p = .004, Exp(B) = 0.96, 95% CI (0.94, 0.99)]. Specifically, for each increase of 1 out of 100 on the MPPRQ scale, a woman is 4% less likely to currently smoke (smoke) tobacco. Maternal-fetal attachment and perceived stress did not predict smoking status. A woman's choice to continue to smoke during pregnancy is complex and personal. Understanding the impact of her personal perception for risk of smoking can assist providers with conversations and improve interventions, leading to increased cessation success and subsequently improve pregnancy and neonatal health outcomes.
