### 重點摘要
兒童及青少年誤食異物是常見且可能導致多種後遺症的狀況，其處理方式從緊急內視鏡取出到觀察等待不等，需根據異物類型、解剖位置、症狀嚴重程度及誤食時間等四個關鍵變數來決定。歐洲小兒胃腸肝病營養學會內視鏡特別興趣小組發表此立場文件，旨在提供一個以實證為基礎，並結合專家意見的異物誤食處理方法。該文件提供清晰、臨床相關的指引和易於使用的演算法，以協助實時決策，並包含專家關於安全有效執行內視鏡移除的實用技巧。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
該文獻填補了在兒童及青少年異物誤食處理上，純粹的實證醫學證據可能不足以指導臨床判斷的空白。它透過整合實證資料與專家意見，提供了一套清晰、實用且具有決策演算法的指引，特別是在異物類型、位置、症狀及時間等變數下，提供即時臨床判斷與安全移除的實用技巧。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Foreign body ingestions in children and adolescents: A position paper of the European Society for Pediatric Gastroenterology, Hepatology, and Nutrition (ESPGHAN) endoscopy special interest group.
**Abstract:** Foreign body (FB) ingestion is a relatively frequent occurrence in the pediatric population with multiple potential sequelae. Appropriate intervention may range from urgent endoscopic retrieval to watchful waiting, with multiple variables influencing the decision. The four key variables include the type of object ingested, the anatomical location of the FB, the presence and severity of symptoms, and the time since ingestion. In this position paper, we provide an evidence-based approach to the management of ingested foreign bodies, incorporating expert opinion where existent evidence alone is insufficient to guide clinical judgement. We offer a clear, clinically relevant guide, with user-friendly algorithms, to assist in real-time decision making. We also include practical tips from experts on how to safely and effectively perform endoscopic removal of an ingested FB.
