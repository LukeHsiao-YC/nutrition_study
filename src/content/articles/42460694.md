### 重點摘要
這項研究旨在描述和比較便秘青少年中，有與沒有排便協同失調(DD)者的肛門直腸測壓(ARM)結果。研究人員回顧了170名青少年，發現40.6%符合DD標準，且DD組的直腸肛門壓力梯度顯著更負。研究提出-45 mmHg作為識別DD的直腸肛門壓力梯度閾值，具有良好的特異性(86%)，並指出DD青少年中憂鬱症的盛行率更高。研究結論指出，直腸肛門壓力梯度是診斷便秘青少年DD的有用參數，尤其在無法進行氣球排出測試時，且應關注此族群的心理健康問題。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這項研究透過回顧性分析，明確了便秘青少年中排便協同失調(DD)的肛門直腸測壓(ARM)特徵，並提出了一個實用的直腸肛門壓力梯度診斷閾值(-45 mmHg)，填補了在青少年族群中DD診斷參數的具體數據空白。然而，由於是回溯性研究，它無法確立憂鬱症與DD之間的因果關係，也未探討介入治療後的成效或長期的預後。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Dyssynergic defecation in adolescents with constipation: Anorectal manometry findings.
**Abstract:** To describe and compare anorectal manometry findings in constipated adolescents with and without dyssynergic defecation (DD). We retrospectively reviewed adolescents ≤18 years who underwent anorectal manometry (ARM) and/or balloon expulsion testing (BET) between 2015 and 2022. Patients with prior pelvic floor therapy, inflammatory bowel disease, or colon surgery were excluded. DD was defined by abnormal ARM (paradoxical anal contraction or inadequate anal relaxation ≤20% during simulated evacuation) and/or abnormal BET (>60 s). Manometric parameters were compared between patients with and without DD and between those with abnormal versus normal BET. Diagnostic performance of rectoanal pressure gradient cutoffs were assessed. A total of 170 adolescents were included; 69 (40.6%) met criteria for DD. Median age was 17 years (interquartile range [IQR] 16-18), and 80.7% were female. Gastrointestinal symptoms did not differ between groups. Patients with DD demonstrated significantly more negative rectoanal pressure gradients compared with those without DD (median [IQR]: -49.0 mmHg [-73.9 to -31.4] vs. -25.2 mmHg [-40.2 to -9.8]; p < 0.001). Rectal sensory thresholds did not differ significantly between groups. A rectoanal pressure gradient cutoff of -45 mmHg demonstrated the best balance of sensitivity (58%) and specificity (86%) for identifying DD. Depression was more prevalent among adolescents with DD (p = 0.007). Rectoanal pressure gradient is a useful manometric parameter for identifying DD in constipated adolescents, particularly when BET is unavailable. Psychological factors such as depression may be more prevalent in this population.
