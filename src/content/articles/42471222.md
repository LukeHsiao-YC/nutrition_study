### 重點摘要
本研究旨在探討運動時間（早晨vs.夜晚）和代謝狀態（空腹vs.進食）如何綜合影響運動後能量攝取與食慾。35名健康年輕人參與隨機交叉試驗，結果顯示，相較於早晨空腹運動，夜晚空腹運動顯著增加運動後急性與24小時能量攝取及食慾，儘管其脂肪氧化率較高。縮短夜晚運動前的禁食時間可減輕急性攝取，但對24小時攝取無效；運動前進食雖抑制脂肪氧化，卻未能降低能量攝取或食慾。研究結論指出，運動後食慾控制主要受運動時間影響，且代謝與行為調節之間存在脫節。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇文獻填補了運動時間和代謝狀態如何綜合影響運動後能量平衡的理解不足，特別是揭示了運動後食慾控制中，運動時間的決定性作用以及代謝與行為調節之間的脫節。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Effects of exercise timing and metabolic state on post-exercise energy intake and appetite - A randomized crossover trial.
**Abstract:** Exercise timing and metabolic state influence post-exercise energy balance, yet their combined effects remain incompletely understood. To disentangle the effects of time of day (morning vs. evening) and nutritional state (fasted vs. fed) on post-exercise energy intake and appetite. In a randomized crossover design, 35 healthy young adults completed three fasted exercise conditions: morning after a 12 h overnight fast (12h AMEx) and evening after a 12 h (12h PMEx) or 6 h fast (6h PMEx). A subsample (n = 17) additionally completed fed morning (AMEx Fed) and fed evening (PMEx Fed) exercise. Each session was 30 min of treadmill running at 65% O Relative to morning (12h AMEx), evening fasted exercise (12h PMEx) increased acute intake by 400 kcal (95% CI: 286, 515) and 24 h intake by 547 kcal (95% CI: 335, 759; both p < 0.001). Shortening the pre-exercise fast from 12 to 6 h before evening exercise attenuated acute but not 24 h intake. Hunger, desire to eat, and prospective consumption were higher, and fullness lower, after evening than morning exercise, mirroring intake. Fat oxidation was greater in fasted and evening conditions; 12h PMEx exceeded 12h AMEx by 0.77 kcal/min (95% CI: 0.37, 1.17; p < 0.001). Pre-exercise feeding suppressed fat oxidation but did not attenuate energy intake or appetite. Evening fasted exercise induces pronounced compensatory eating and elevated 24 h appetite, despite greater fat oxidation. Pre-exercise feeding alters substrate use but does not reduce intake, underscoring a dissociation between metabolic and behavioral regulation and the primacy of timing in post-exercise appetite control. This trial was registered at ClinicalTrials.gov (NCT06947824; https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06947824).
