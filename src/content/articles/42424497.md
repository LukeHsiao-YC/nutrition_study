### 重點摘要
縱向研究常面臨群組設計雖有效率卻不實際，而重複橫斷面設計（RCS）精確度不足的問題。本研究提出DISC（不同個體，相同群集）設計，這是一種混合抽樣方案，在基準線採樣一次群集，但在不同時間點於相同群集內抽取不同個體樣本。分析與模擬結果顯示，DISC設計能顯著提高差異中的差異（DID）估計量的精確度，其變異數相較於RCS設計大幅降低，尤其在存在群集效應時。因此，DISC設計能有效提升縱向估計量的統計效率。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇文獻填補了在縱向研究中，當傳統世代追蹤設計因不切實際而無法採用時，如何透過創新的抽樣策略（DISC設計）顯著提升估計量精確度的研究空白。它提供了一個兼顧實用性與統計效率的替代方案，介於傳統世代設計與重複橫斷面設計之間。未來的研究可能需進一步探討DISC設計在更複雜或特定人口群體中的實施挑戰與效果評估。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Repeated sampling of different individuals within the same clusters to improve precision of longitudinal estimators: the DISC design.
**Abstract:** Longitudinal studies often involve repeated cluster sampling of a population at multiple time points, such as in difference-in-differences (DID) studies. Although cohort designs typically lead to more efficient estimators relative to repeated cross-sectional (RCS) designs, they are often impractical. We describe the DISC (Different Individuals, Same Clusters) design, a sampling scheme that improves the precision of estimators in these settings. The DISC design represents a hybrid between a cohort and an RCS design, in which the researcher takes a single sample of clusters at baseline, but takes different cross-sectional samples of individuals within clusters at each time point. We show analytically that the DISC design yields DID estimators with much higher precision relative to an RCS design, particularly if cluster effects are present. For example, for a design with two surveys, 40 clusters, and 25 individuals per cluster, the variance of a commonly used DID treatment effect estimator is 2.3 times higher in the RCS design for an intraclass correlation coefficient (ICC) of 0.05 and 3.8 times higher for an ICC of 0.1. We also present results of a simulation study comparing the RCS and DISC designs, using both a simple DID estimator and a more complex doubly-robust DID estimator that leverages covariate information, and show gains in precision for both estimators when using the DISC design. Additionally, we illustrate DISC sampling using a household survey dataset from South Africa. Use of the DISC design can result in estimators that have substantially lower variance than the analogous estimator resulting from an RCS design.
