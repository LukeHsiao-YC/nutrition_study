### 重點摘要
一項針對MIND飲食的隨機試驗曾因依從性變異，未能顯示飲食干預對認知結果有顯著影響。本研究為該試驗的事後分析，旨在探討經血漿生物標誌物驗證的MIND飲食依從性，是否與全球認知的縱向變化相關。研究分析了590名65-84歲的社區成人，依MIND飲食分數變化將依從性分為無、中度、顯著改善三類。結果顯示，MIND飲食依從性有顯著改善（分數平均增加4點）的參與者，其全球認知複合分數每年顯著增加，且血漿葉黃素-玉米黃素和β-胡蘿蔔素水平較高，而中度改善者則無顯著變化。這表明，提高MIND飲食依從性與全球認知功能的顯著改善相關，澄清了原始試驗的結果。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
本研究填補了原始MIND試驗因飲食依從性變異而未能顯示認知益處的空白，證實了當受試者確實達到更高的MIND飲食依從性時，其全球認知功能確實能獲得顯著改善。這也說明了在評估飲食干預效果時，依從性的重要性。未來的研究可進一步探討如何有效提升並維持飲食依從性，並評估不同介入策略的效果。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Adherence to the MIND Diet and Longitudinal Changes in Global Cognition: A Post-Hoc Analysis of the MIND Trial.
**Abstract:** A three-year randomized trial comparing the Mediterranean-DASH Intervention for Neurodegenerative Delay (MIND) diet with a control diet found no significant differences in cognitive outcomes by intervention assignment, potentially due to variability in dietary adherence. We examined whether adherence to the MIND diet, validated using plasma nutrient biomarkers, was associated with longitudinal changes in global cognition. The MIND trial enrolled 604 community-dwelling adults aged 65-84 years without cognitive impairment at baseline. MIND diet adherence was quantified as the change in MIND scores from baseline, with higher values indicating greater adherence, and validated using blood levels of antioxidant nutrients, including lutein-zeaxanthin and β-carotene. Participants were classified into categories of no, moderate, or substantial improvement in MIND diet score. Linear mixed-effects models with random intercepts and slopes were used to estimate annual changes in global cognition. Of 590 participants (mean age 70.3 years and 65% females) included in the analysis, 116 (19.7%) showed no improvement, 224 (38.0%) modest improvement, and 250 (42.3%) substantial improvement in their MIND diet score. Compared with participants with no change in MIND score, those with greater improvement (mean absolute increase of 4 points on the 0-14 scale) demonstrated a significant annual increase in global cognitive composite scores (β = 0.035; 95% CI: 0.006, 0.064), along with higher plasma lutein-zeaxanthin (β = 0.088; 95% CI: 0.038, 0.137) and β-carotene (β = 0.075; 95% CI: 0.001, 0.150). Modest improvement was not associated with significant changes in cognitive function. The associations between changes in MIND score and cognition were not modified by dietary intervention arm (p-for-interactions > 0.167). Participants with greater increases in MIND diet scores from baseline, indicating higher adherence, showed significant improvements in global cognitive function compared with those whose scores did not increase during the trial. Clinical Trial Registry (clinicaltrials.gov) number: NCT02817074.
