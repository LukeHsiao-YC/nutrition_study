### 重點摘要
這項研究旨在解決幼兒期中高強度身體活動（MVPA）促進策略不明的問題，其主要原因在於影響因素複雜且多樣。研究團隊開發了一個代理基模型（ABM），用於模擬3-9歲兒童在不同環境下的MVPA，並結合了文獻理論與高品質的隨機對照試驗數據。結果顯示，該模型能精確重現真實世界的MVPA模式，揭示了影響MVPA的關鍵路徑和環境。此ABM為探索兒童MVPA的成因提供了一個強大工具，並有助於制定促進幼兒身體活動的實踐和政策，為終身健康奠定基礎。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇文獻填補了由於影響兒童中高強度身體活動（MVPA）的因素眾多、動態且異質，導致目前缺乏有效策略來持續支持兒童MVPA的知識空白。透過建立一個能模擬複雜交互作用的計算模型，研究為探索MVPA的複雜成因提供了一個新穎的工具，有助於未來制定基於證據的介入措施與政策。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** What Makes Them Move? An Agent-Based Model of Childhood Physical Activity.
**Abstract:** Engaging in moderate-to-vigorous physical activity (MVPA) in early childhood can have both immediate and long-term health benefits. Strategies for consistently supporting this are currently unknown, largely due to the vast number of potentially interrelated and dynamic contributing factors that may also be heterogenous across children. We developed an agent-based model (ABM) that represents children ages 3-9 who can engage in MVPA in a variety of settings in which they spend time. Our model incorporates key theoretical constructs identified in the literature and is grounded in high-quality empirical evidence, primarily participant data from a cohort-based randomized controlled trial with extensive longitudinal accelerometry measurement. We assess the ABM's ability to reproduce patterns of MVPA observed in the cohort.
