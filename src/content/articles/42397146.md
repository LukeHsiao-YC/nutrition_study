### 重點摘要
食道切除術患者常面臨術後體重減輕及營養不良，但缺乏明確的術後BMI目標。本回顧性世代研究分析了317名食道癌患者在微創食道切除術後3、6、12個月的BMI，以識別與死亡、非計畫性再入院或需門診治療肺炎等不良結果相關的BMI閾值。研究發現術後BMI為19.5 kg/m²是與不良結果相關的閾值，BMI低於此值的患者面臨不良結果的風險顯著增高。這項研究結果強調了食道癌患者術後維持BMI在19.5 kg/m²或以上對於改善預後的重要性。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇文獻成功填補了食道癌患者食道切除術後，缺乏明確術後BMI目標值的研究空白，首次確立了19.5 kg/m²作為與不良預後相關的BMI閾值。然而，作為回顧性研究，其結果可能受限於數據可用性，未來的研究可評估基於此閾值的介入措施在改善患者預後上的有效性。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Postoperative body mass index and the risk of unfavorable outcomes in adults following esophagectomy for esophageal cancer: A retrospective cohort study.
**Abstract:** Esophagectomy is an invasive procedure, frequently complicated by long-term weight loss and nutritional deficiencies. Although outpatient nutritional support is recommended, specific target body mass index (BMI) has not been established. Accordingly, we aimed to identify the threshold of postoperative BMI associated with unfavorable outcomes following esophagectomy. We analyzed 317 patients who underwent minimally invasive esophagectomy for esophageal cancer, measuring their postoperative BMI at 3, 6, and 12 months. Unfavorable outcomes were defined as a composite of mortality, unplanned readmission, or pneumonia requiring outpatient treatment. Restricted cubic spline models were employed to determine the BMI threshold, and multivariable generalized estimating equation logistic regression was used to examine the significance of the threshold on unfavorable outcomes.
