### 重點摘要
這項研究旨在探討不同植物性飲食模式與肥胖相關癌症發生率之間的關聯，特別強調區分健康與不健康的植物性食物。研究追蹤了16,256名參與者，透過食物頻率問卷評估其整體、健康及不健康的植物性飲食指數，並使用Cox模型進行分析。在中位數13年的追蹤期後，確認了221例肥胖相關癌症病例。結果顯示，整體植物性飲食和不健康植物性飲食模式與癌症風險無關，但飲食中健康植物性食物攝取量最高的參與者，其肥胖相關癌症的風險顯著較低。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇文獻填補了過去研究中未能充分區分「健康」與「不健康」植物性飲食模式對肥胖相關癌症風險影響的空白，明確指出植物性飲食的品質才是關鍵因素，而不僅僅是攝取植物性食物本身。

**Abstract:** Evidence suggests that plant-based dietary patterns -favoring plant-based foods without being strictly vegetarian or vegan - may prevent obesity and offer health benefits. Since not all plant-based foods are equally healthy, it is essential to distinguish between healthful and unhealthful plant-based options. Objective: This study aimed to analyze the associations between different plant-based dietary patterns and the incidence of obesity-related cancers. We followed-up 16,256 participants from the "Seguimiento Universidad de Navarra" (SUN) prospective cohort. Based on a validated food frequency questionnaire, we calculated adapted overall, healthful and unhealthful plant-based diet index scores. Scores for were assessed at baseline and repeatedly after 10 years of follow-up. Hazard ratios (HR) and 95% confidence intervals (CIs) were estimated using multivariable-adjusted time-dependent Cox models. After a median follow-up of 13 years, 221 incident obesity-related cancer cases were confirmed. Overall plant-based and unhealthful plant-based dietary patterns showed no association.
