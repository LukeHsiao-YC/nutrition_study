### 重點摘要
母乳是早產兒推薦的餵養標準，但奈及利亞新生兒加護病房（NICU）對此類實踐的證據有限。本回顧研究系統性檢視了奈及利亞NICU母乳利用的實踐、認知與經驗，共納入15篇文獻。結果顯示，儘管母乳受到重視，但在哺乳支持、擠奶實踐、母乳強化及捐贈母乳的使用方面存在重大知識空白。鑑於這些關鍵缺失與奈及利亞高比例的早產兒，未來需要更多研究來優化NICU餵養實踐。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇文獻揭示了奈及利亞新生兒加護病房在哺乳支持、擠奶實踐、嬰兒餵養方案以及捐贈母乳可用性上的關鍵文獻空白。研究限制在於其僅檢視現有發表文獻，未進行實地觀察或干預，因此呼籲未來需進行更多實證研究以填補這些知識鴻溝並改善實踐。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Exploring the Experience of Human Milk Use in Nigeria Neonatal Intensive Care Units: A Scoping Review.
**Abstract:** Human milk is the recommended feeding standard for preterm and low-birth-weight infants. The 2022 World Health Organization (WHO) guidance prioritizes mother's milk, donor human milk (DHM) when mother's milk is insufficient, early feeding initiation, and potential fortification for the smallest infants. However, evidence on current practices and alignment with these recommendations in Nigeria is limited. This scoping review examined human milk utilization practices, perceptions, and experiences in Nigerian neonatal intensive care units (NICUs) populations. A systematic search was conducted in March 2025 following PRISMA guidelines using PubMed and African Journals Online. Articles from 2008 onward were included to align with WHO resolutions on DHM use. Eligible studies were original research or reviews from Nigeria addressing infant feeding, milk pumping or storage, lactation support, infant health outcomes, and attitudes toward DHM. Of 137 records identified, 15 studies (2008-2025) met the inclusion criteria. Human milk was valued for NICU feeding, yet major knowledge gaps persist regarding recommended practices. There was a dearth of information related to lactation support and milk expression practices. Only one study mentioned fortification, and no included studies reported the use of donor human milk. Awareness of DHM among mothers was low, though willingness to donate exceeded willingness to receive. Critical gaps in the published literature exist regarding lactation support, milk expression practices, infant feeding protocols, and DHM availability in Nigerian NICUs. Given the high rates and numbers of preterm births in Nigeria annually, more research into NICU feeding practices is warranted.
