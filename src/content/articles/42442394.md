### 重點摘要
本研究旨在探討印度低出生體重 (LBW) 的趨勢、決定因素及其對兒童早期成長的影響，因過去較少證據探討出生體重如何影響早期兒童成長。作者利用印度國家家庭健康調查的數據，分析了母體、家庭和健康相關因素與LBW及成長遲緩的關聯。結果顯示，低出生體重盛行率經調整後高達40%，且低出生體重兒童體重過輕和發育遲緩的機率更高，但一旦兒童出生體重過輕，其後續預防生長發育不良的潛力有限。研究強調母體營養在出生體重和兒童成長中的主導作用，並建議應優先改善女孩的營養狀況，以打破營養不良的代際循環。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇文獻填補了關於出生體重如何影響兒童早期成長結果的證據空白。研究結果表明，一旦兒童出生體重過輕，其後續預防體格發育失敗的潛力有限，這可能指示了在出生後階段干預的局限性。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Trends, determinants, and anthropometric consequences of low birthweight in Indian children: a prospective analysis of national data.
**Abstract:** Low birthweight (LBW) is a major concern for child survival and growth, and India accounts for the highest number of such cases globally. Studies have examined determinants of birthweight and child growth but little evidence exists on how birthweight influences growth outcomes during early childhood. This paper examines associations between maternal, household, and health-related factors, LBW, and growth faltering, and explores childhood growth trajectories across birthweight groups. Data from three waves of India's National Family Health Survey were used to examine heaping-adjusted trends and predictors of LBW. Regression-based decomposition was used to quantify the way in which changes in predictors could reduce future LBW prevalence. Trajectories of childhood anthropometry by LBW status and determinants were examined by using smoothing and regression methods. The LBW prevalence between 2000 and 2020 was 20% (unadjusted) and 40% (heaping-adjusted). Improvements in maternal stature, weight, household wealth, and antenatal care coverage could yield the largest future reductions in LBW. Children born with LBW had 1.5 times higher odds of being underweight and 1.3 times higher odds of stunting. Maternal short stature, underweight, and poverty were associated with higher odds of subsequent anthropometric faltering regardless of LBW. Findings show the dominant role of maternal nutrition in birthweight and childhood growth outcomes. Once a child is born with LBW, there is limited potential to prevent subsequent anthropometric failure. To break the intergenerational cycle of undernutrition, programs must prioritize improved nutrition for girls beginning early in life and sustained through adolescence and preconception.
