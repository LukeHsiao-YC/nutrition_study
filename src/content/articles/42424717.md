### 重點摘要
這篇範疇性回顧旨在探討囊狀纖維化 (CF) 兒童在調節劑時代的飲食組成與品質，以應對其不斷變化的營養需求。研究採用 Joanna Briggs Institute 方法學，搜尋了多個資料庫，最終納入56項研究，涵蓋4977名CF兒童。結果顯示，大多數研究著重於飲食組成（尤其是能量和巨量營養素），而較少評估飲食品質。評估飲食品質的研究報告顯示，CF兒童的乳製品、穀物和肉類攝取量高，但蔬菜、全穀物、豆類、堅果、雞蛋和魚類攝取量低，飲食多樣性不足，且與最佳飲食的符合度為低至中等。研究建議未來的兒科CF飲食研究應超越能量和巨量營養素，更深入地探討飲食品質及其影響因素。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
本回顧指出，過去兒科囊狀纖維化飲食研究過於側重於飲食組成（能量和巨量營養素），而對飲食品質的評估有限。此外，納入使用囊狀纖維化跨膜傳導調節劑（CFTR調節劑）兒童的研究數量稀少，且針對年幼、胰腺功能正常兒童和設有對照組的研究數量不足。因此，未來的研究應擴展到更全面地描述飲食品質及影響兒童飲食選擇的因素。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Diet composition and quality in children with cystic fibrosis in the modulator era: A scoping review.
**Abstract:** With improved survival and changing nutritional priorities in cystic fibrosis (CF), diet quality is increasingly relevant to reduce chronic disease risks and support gastrointestinal and overall health. This scoping review aimed to identify and describe the range and characteristics of literature examining diet composition and quality in children with CF in the CF transmembrane conductance regulator (CFTR) modulator era, and identify evidence gaps to inform future research and guidelines. Joanna Briggs Institute methodology was used. The protocol was published on Open Science Framework. Ovid MEDLINE, CINAHL and EMBASE were searched from 2012-January 2026. We included primary research studies reporting oral dietary intake in children with CF using dietary assessment methods. Two reviewers independently screened articles. Data were extracted on study characteristics, populations, dietary assessment methods, outcomes, and key findings. Fifty-six studies were included, encompassing 4977 children with CF. Most studies examined diet composition, particularly energy and macronutrient intake; fewer assessed diet quality. Studies assessing diet quality reported high intakes of dairy, cereals and meat, low intakes of vegetables, wholegrains, legumes, nuts, eggs and fish, low dietary diversity, and low to moderate concordance with optimal diets. Only thirteen studies included children taking CFTR modulators. Younger and pancreatic sufficient children, and studies with a control group, were under-represented. Dietary research in paediatric CF has focused on diet composition, with limited assessment of diet quality. Future research should extend beyond energy and macronutrients to better characterise diet quality and factors influencing dietary choices in children with CF.
