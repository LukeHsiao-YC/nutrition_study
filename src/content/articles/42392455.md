### 重點摘要
本研究旨在探討紅血球脂肪酸與敵意及A型人格特質之間的長期關聯，因為這些人格特質與心血管疾病預後相關。研究追蹤了以色列基布茲家庭成員約10年，在基線測量紅血球脂肪酸水平，並在後續測量人格特質，同時調整了生活方式、社會人口學和多基因背景。結果顯示，較高的紅血球總n-6脂肪酸水平與較低的A型人格分數相關，而總n-3和總n-6與敵意之間的關聯在調整基線人格分數後有所減弱。這些發現支持進一步研究脂肪酸生物學與人格特質的關係，但強調需採用更適合因果推論的研究設計。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇文獻填補了對紅血球脂肪酸與長期人格特質之間關聯的理解空白，並在考量遺傳背景下進行了探索。然而，本研究為觀察性設計，無法確立因果關係，未來的研究限制在於需要更嚴謹的實驗或介入設計來驗證脂肪酸與人格特質之間的因果關聯。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Associations of red blood cell fatty acids with personality traits: 10-year follow-up in the Kibbutzim Family Study.
**Abstract:** The ability of hostility and Type-A personality to predict cardiovascular outcomes makes understanding antecedents of these personality traits an important public health objective. We examined whether blood-measured fatty acids (the exposure) are associated with hostility and Type-A personality (the outcome), while accounting for lifestyle, sociodemographic factors and polygenic background. Personality traits, sociodemographic and lifestyle data were obtained in 1992-93 from 452 family members living in kibbutz settlements in Israel (Visit 1) and re-measured 8-10 years later in 379 individuals (Visit 2). Red blood cell fatty acid levels were determined in Visit 1 by gas chromatography. Longitudinal associations of Visit 1 fatty acids with Visit 2 personality traits were examined using linear models, before and after controlling for baseline personality scores. The contribution of the environmental factors to personality scores beyond heritability was estimated by variance decomposition. In longitudinal analysis, 1% increase in Visit 1 Total n-6 fatty acid was associated with a 0.328-unit decrease in Visit 2 Type-A score (95% confidence interval (CI): -0.571, -0.085). After adjustment for baseline personality levels, the association was slightly attenuated (β=-0.204; CI: -0.399, -0.009). One-percent higher Total n-6 was also associated with 1.119 units lower Visit 2 hostility score (CI: -2.777, 0.038; p=0.055). Finally, independent of the genetic contribution (32%-38% of adjusted variability in hostility and Type-A personality), a 1% increase in Total n-3 and Total n-6 was associated with 2.539 (CI: -3.907, -1.171) and 0.201 (CI: -0.365, -0.037) unit lower hostility and Type-A scores, respectively. Higher RBC Total n-6 fatty acid levels were associated with lower Type-A personality scores. After adjustment for baseline personality levels, the associations between Total n-6 and hostility and between Total n-3 and hostility were attenuated. These findings support further investigation of the relationship between fatty acid biology and personality traits using study designs better suited to causal inference.
