### 重點摘要
不必要的術後抗生素使用是常見問題。本研究採階梯式分段群集隨機試驗，於9家美國兒童醫院評估修改電子健康記錄醫囑集加上抗微生物管理團隊工作坊（實驗組）是否比單獨修改醫囑集（對照組）更能減少不當術後抗生素使用時間。儘管原始數據顯示實驗組在不當術後抗生素使用率上略低，但經廣義估計方程分析，兩組在主要研究終點（術後超過24小時的不當抗生素使用持續時間）上並無統計學顯著差異（P=.74）。此外，兩組在任何術後抗生素使用、手術部位感染或困難梭狀芽孢桿菌感染等次要終點上亦無差異，研究結論指出額外的促進式工作坊並未帶來顯著成效。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
本研究發現，單純修改電子健康記錄醫囑集已具一定效果，但額外的促進式工作坊並未能進一步減少不當術後抗生素使用。這表明未來的研究需要更深入地理解醫院的具體情境與環境因素如何影響變革策略的實施與成效，以填補目前在設計和執行更有效抗微生物管理干預措施方面的知識空白。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Eliminating Inappropriate Postoperative Antibiotics: A Stepped-Wedge Cluster Randomized Trial.
**Abstract:** Unnecessary postoperative antibiotic use is common. This study evaluated whether modifying electronic health record order sets plus participation in a facilitation workshop by the antimicrobial stewardship team reduced inappropriate postoperative antibiotic duration more than modifying order sets alone. A stepped-wedge cluster randomized trial compared order set modification (active control) vs order set modification plus facilitation (experimental) from November 1, 2020, to October 31, 2023, at 9 US children's hospitals. The primary outcome was the rate of inappropriate postoperative antibiotic duration of greater than 24 hours after incision closure. Secondary outcomes included inappropriate postoperative antibiotics, defined as any antibiotic use and the rate of surgical site infections (SSI) or Clostridioides difficile infections (CDI). Generalized estimating equations (GEEs) accounting for clustering at the step and hospital level were utilized to compare 2 groups. A total of 18 267 patients (5910 control, 12 357 experimental) were included, with a median age of 9.2 years (IQR 2-14), 50.6% of the female gender, and 58% with public insurance/self-pay. The primary outcome was lower in the experimental group (10.05%, (95% CI: 9.53-10.59) than the control (11.02%, 95% CI: 10.23-11.84, P = .05), but this difference was not apparent in the GEE analysis (P = .74). The secondary outcomes demonstrated no difference between control and experimental groups for any postoperative antibiotic use (45.1% vs 44.7%, P = .98), SSI (1.96% vs 2.03%, P = .70), and CDI (0.15% vs 0.06%, P = .85). Facilitation training did not reduce inappropriate postoperative antibiotic duration compared with order set modification; better understanding of how hospital context informs change strategies is needed.
