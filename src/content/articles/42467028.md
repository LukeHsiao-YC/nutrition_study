### 重點摘要
本研究旨在評估飲食多樣性介入措施對減輕妊娠期困擾的有效性。這是一項單盲隨機臨床試驗，將84名懷孕12至16週的孕婦隨機分為介入組（接受8週飲食多樣性教育）和對照組。結果顯示，介入組在調整後的飲食多樣性分數（DDS）和妊娠困擾量表（TPDS）平均值方面與對照組存在顯著差異（P < 0.001），且DDS的變化與TPDS的變化呈顯著負相關。研究證實飲食多樣性教育計畫能顯著降低妊娠期困擾並提升飲食多樣性，對於欠發達地區孕婦的全面健康倡議至關重要。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇文獻填補了在欠發達地區，透過飲食多樣性教育介入來減輕孕期困擾的實證研究空白，為當地孕婦的全面健康倡議提供了重要的支持證據。雖然研究結果具參考價值，但其範圍侷限於特定地區，未來研究可考慮擴大地理範圍或增加樣本量，以提升結果的普適性。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** The Role of Dietary Diversity in Mitigating Pregnancy Distress: A Randomized Clinical Trial.
**Abstract:** This study aimed to evaluate the effectiveness of a dietary diversity intervention in reducing pregnancy distress. A single-blind randomized clinical trial with 2 parallel groups. Health centers in Birjand city. Pregnant women between 12 and 16 weeks of gestation were recruited for this study and randomly assigned to either the intervention group (n = 42) or the control group (n = 42). The intervention contained dietary diversity education for 8 weeks. The control group received standard antenatal care (monthly check-ups) and general nutritional guidance for pregnancy. Data were collected at baseline and end line using the Tilburg Pregnancy Distress Scale (TPDS) and Minimum Dietary Diversity for Women. Comparing the 2 groups at baseline (independent t test and chi-square test of independence), and at the end line (paired sample t test and analysis of covariance were conducted). P < 0.05 was considered statistically significant. We found significant differences between the 2 groups regarding adjusted mean values of dietary diversity score (DDS) and TPDS, controlled for baseline values (P < 0.001). Changes in DDS were significantly correlated with changes in TPDS (r = -0.41; P < 0.01). The results of this study demonstrated a significant reduction in pregnancy distress and an enhanced DDS within the intervention group. These findings indicate that such educational programs could be essential to comprehensive health initiatives for pregnant women in underdeveloped contexts.
