### 重點摘要
德國一項針對超過62萬名新生兒的試驗性篩檢研究，評估了新生兒篩檢尿素循環障礙 (UCDs) 的可行性、診斷品質及臨床效益。研究發現此篩檢具有高靈敏度和特異性，確診了19例UCDs，儘管超過半數確診者在篩檢報告出來前已出現症狀。結果顯示，篩檢能早期診斷並降低死亡率，但對於早期發作的代謝失代償預防仍具挑戰。研究結論指出新生兒篩檢UCDs在技術上可行，並建議進一步優化篩檢演算法以提高特異性及臨床效益。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這項研究在大規模試驗中驗證了新生兒篩檢尿素循環障礙 (UCDs) 的可行性及其降低死亡率的臨床效益，填補了此類篩檢在實際應用和臨床結果評估上的空白。然而，研究也指出篩檢報告前已有半數以上確診個案出現症狀，且未能有效預防早期代謝失代償，同時篩檢的陽性預測值 (PPV) 較低。未來的研究應著重於優化篩檢演算法，例如整合代謝物比率，以提高特異性、PPV並改善早期臨床效益。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Newborn Screening for Urea Cycle Disorders and Clinical Outcomes.
**Abstract:** To evaluate feasibility and diagnostic and process qualities as well as the clinical benefit of newborn screening (NBS) for urea cycle disorders (UCDs). Between 2016 and 2023, 624 480 neonates were enrolled in the German NBS pilot study, including NBS for UCDs by tandem mass spectrometry. In addition, patients with confirmed cases were enrolled in an observational, multicenter outcome study assessing clinical and neurodevelopmental outcomes. NBS for UCDs demonstrated high sensitivity (100%) and specificity (99.988%) with a positive predictive value (PPV) of 0.2. Nineteen cases (7 ornithine transcarbamoylase deficiency, 7 argininosuccinate synthetase 1 deficiency, 4 argininosuccinate lyase deficiency, and 1 carbamoylphosphate synthetase 1 deficiency) were confirmed, yielding a cumulative birth prevalence of 1:32 867 newborns. Despite short process times, more than 50% of screened individuals with a UCD were already symptomatic at first NBS report. Retrospective analyses indicated that integration of metabolite ratios (eg, ornithine/citrulline) may further enhance PPV. Outcome data from 25 individuals revealed higher rates of metabolic decompensations and hospitalizations in mitochondrial UCDs vs cytosolic forms. At last follow-up, 55% showed disease-related symptoms, and mean IQ was reduced in symptomatic individuals, but mortality was lower than previously reported. NBS for UCDs is technically feasible and enables early diagnosis. Although early-onset decompensations remain difficult to prevent, screening reduces mortality. Refinement of the screening algorithm may further improve specificity and clinical benefit.
