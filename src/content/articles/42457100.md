### 重點摘要
這項研究針對4,809名青少年進行了為期兩年的追蹤，旨在探討問題性社群媒體使用（PSMU）軌跡與認知表現之間的關聯。研究識別出三種PSMU軌跡：無/低度增加、中低度增加和中高度增加。結果顯示，與無/低度增加軌跡的青少年相比，中低度和中高度PSMU軌跡的青少年在圖像序列記憶測驗和圖片詞彙測驗中均表現出顯著較低的得分。這項發現指出，問題性社群媒體使用的增加與青少年記憶和語言領域的認知表現下降有關，強調了開發有效介入措施的重要性。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
本研究透過識別問題性社群媒體使用（PSMU）的縱向軌跡，並將其與青少年記憶和語言領域的認知表現下降明確關聯，填補了對PSMU模式如何影響青少年認知發展的長期性理解空白。然而，該研究並未深入探討PSMU影響認知表現的具體神經生理或心理機制，也未評估任何潛在介入措施的有效性。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Problematic social media use trajectories are linked with lower cognitive performance in adolescents.
**Abstract:** Adolescence is a critical period of ongoing cognitive and behavioral development. Problematic social media use (PSMU) is a potentially important factor shaping cognitive development during this period. This study examined associations between PSMU trajectories and cognitive performance. A total of 4,809 adolescents (mean age 12 years; 48.9% female; 73.6% White) from the Adolescent Brain Cognitive Development Study were assessed from Year 2 (2018-2020) to Year 4 (2020-2022). Group-based trajectory modeling estimated patterns of PSMU of adolescents aged 10-16 years. Cognition was assessed at Year 4 (aged 12-16 years) using five of the seven subtests of the National Institutes of Health Toolbox Cognition Battery. Multiple linear regression models estimated associations between PSMU trajectories and cognitive performance, adjusting for demographics, cognitive scores, mental health symptoms, and study site. Three PSMU trajectories were identified: no/low increasing, low-to-moderate, and moderate-to-high. Compared with adolescents in the no/low increasing trajectory, adolescents in the low-to-moderate trajectory demonstrated lower scores on the Picture Sequence Memory Test (β [standardized beta]: -0.10, 95% CI [confidence interval]: -0.16, -0.03, p-value=0.003) and Picture Vocabulary Test (β: -0.11, 95% CI: -0.16, -0.07, p-value<0.001). Adolescents in the moderate-to-high trajectory demonstrated lower scores on the Picture Sequence Memory Test (β: -0.24, 95% CI: -0.35, -0.13, p-value<0.001) and Picture Vocabulary Test (β: -0.17, 95% CI: -0.25, -0.09, p-value<0.001). Increasing trajectories of PSMU were associated with lower cognitive performance in memory and language domains, highlighting the importance of developing effective interventions to address PSMU behaviors and support cognitive development.
