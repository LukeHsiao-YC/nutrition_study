### 重點摘要
這項研究旨在比較患有和未患有自閉症譜系障礙 (ASD) 兒童的慢性便秘治療結果，以填補此領域的比較數據空白。研究回溯性分析了196名兒童的數據，發現ASD組兒童的男性比例較高，藥物依從性顯著較低，且在最終追蹤時便秘解決率僅為11%，遠低於非ASD組的25%。此外，ASD組中注意力缺陷多動障礙 (ADHD) 的共病率更高。研究結論指出，ASD兒童的治療依從性較差，便秘預後也較差，因此需要針對性的多學科策略來改善這高風險群體的治療依從性和結果。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
本研究填補了過去對於患有與未患有自閉症譜系障礙兒童慢性便秘的臨床特徵、管理和治療結果缺乏比較數據的空白，尤其是在自閉症嚴重程度不同組別間的比較。然而，作為回溯性研究，其可能存在數據完整性與因果推論的限制，且研究來自三級轉診服務，結果推廣至一般社區或初級照護的適用性可能有限。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Constipation outcomes in children with and without autism spectrum disorder: Insights from a tertiary motility service.
**Abstract:** Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) is commonly associated with gastrointestinal comorbidities, particularly chronic constipation. Despite high clinical burden and frequent tertiary referrals, comparative data describing clinical characteristics, management and outcomes between children with and without ASD-and across ASD severity levels-remain limited. This study compared constipation outcomes at 1 year and the most recent follow-up between children with and without ASD and assessed whether autism severity influenced prognosis. We retrospectively analysed children with chronic constipation managed through a tertiary motility service and enrolled as of 31 January 2024. Demographics, comorbidities, adherence, interventions and outcomes were extracted. Subgroup analyses examined outcomes across ASD severity levels. Statistical comparisons used Chi-square, Mann-Whitney U and Kruskal-Wallis tests, with significance defined as p < 0.05. Among 196 children, 74 (37.7%) had ASD and 122 (62.2%) did not. The ASD cohort had more males (62.2% vs. 45.9%, p = 0.038), lower medication adherence (56.8% vs. 76.2%, p = 0.006) and lower constipation resolution at last follow-up (11% vs. 25%, p = 0.036). Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) was more prevalent in the ASD group (50% vs. 13.9%, p = 0.0001). Across ASD severity levels, outcomes, compliance, and therapy needs were broadly similar, though toileting compliance tended to be lowest in Level 3. Children with ASD demonstrate lower treatment adherence and poorer constipation resolution compared with non-ASD peers. Outcomes across ASD severity levels are largely similar. Targeted, multidisciplinary strategies are needed to address adherence and optimise outcomes in this high-risk group.
