### 重點摘要
坦尚尼亞恩約姆地區儘管持續投入多部門資源，兒童發育遲緩盛行率仍居高不下，從2014年的53%僅微幅下降至2022年的50%。本文認為，介入措施高覆蓋率與人體測量學影響有限之間的差距，歸因於當前計畫在結構和概念上的局限，而非努力或政治意願不足。文章分析了將發育遲緩混淆為短期成功指標、缺乏生化評估、資金週期與生物恢復時間錯位，以及社區與臨床服務脫節等四大機制。為此，作者建議將發育遲緩視為基於生物學的適應性過程，並提出整合簡單生化篩查、重新設計監測框架及強化轉診系統的建議。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇文獻指出當前發育遲緩介入計畫在概念和結構上的局限，特別是忽略了生物學異質性、時間尺度不匹配及服務碎片化等問題，為現有模式的有效性提供了批判性視角。文章同時明確指出，對於某些機制（如生物學異質性）的證據基礎仍有限，需進一步研究驗證。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** High Coverage, Limited Impact: Rethinking the Effectiveness of Intensive Stunting Reduction Interventions in Njombe Region, Tanzania.
**Abstract:** Despite sustained, multisectoral investment in stunting reduction, childhood stunting prevalence in Tanzania's Njombe region has remained persistently elevated, declining only modestly from approximately 53% in 2014 to 50% by 2022. This perspective argues that the gap between intervention coverage and anthropometric impact reflects structural and conceptual limitations in current programs rather than an absence of effort or political will. Four interrelated mechanisms are examined: the conflation of stunting a lagging cumulative outcome indicator with short-term program success criteria; near exclusive reliance on anthropometric monitoring without biochemical assessment, which obscure the biological heterogeneity underlying growth faltering; the misalignment between short funding cycles and the biological time scales required for growth recovery; and fragmentation between community-based prevention and clinical nutrition services. Drawing on Njombe-specific evidence alongside global implementation literature, this paper contributes to a growing call to reframe stunting reduction as a biologically informed, adaptive process. Where the evidence base is limited, these are explicitly identified as hypotheses warranting further investigation. The recommendations advanced targeted integration of simple biochemical screening, redesigned monitoring frameworks that capture intermediate biological outcomes, and strengthened referral systems, which are practical, contextually grounded and aligned with Tanzania's existing health system architecture.
