### 重點摘要
本研究旨在探討肺癌篩檢中共享決策（SDM）的實施現況與潛在挑戰，因為SDM對於知情參與至關重要，但其實施方式仍不明確。研究採用混合方法，結合了文獻回顧（分析35篇文獻）和質性訪談（對22位醫護人員與13位潛在篩檢參與者進行），探討了SDM的應用現況、參與者偏好及實施障礙。結果顯示，SDM雖能提升知識與滿意度，但在實務中常僅限於資訊提供；參與者對決策角色偏好各異，而醫護人員則面臨時間與訓練不足等挑戰。研究結論建議肺癌篩檢計劃應提供彈性決策支持，並允許SDM透過多種途徑和專業角色進行，以滿足個別化需求，避免形式化實施。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇文獻填補了肺癌篩檢中共享決策（SDM）如何有效且有意義地實施的研究空白，特別是在尚未全面推行篩檢計畫的「前實施階段」情境下。儘管SDM被認為有益，但其實際應用常受限於資訊提供而非深度討論，本研究探討了實施SDM所面臨的障礙及參與者的多樣化需求，提供了實務實施的具體建議。未來的研究可能需要在已建立的篩檢計畫中驗證這些發現，並進一步探索將SDM融入現有照護路徑的有效策略。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Shared decision-making for lung cancer screening in a pre-implementation context: A mixed-methods study.
**Abstract:** Lung cancer screening with low-dose computed tomography is increasingly recommended as a strategy to reduce lung cancer mortality. In the context of such screening programmes, shared decision-making (SDM) is essential for informed participation, yet how it should be meaningfully implemented remains unclear. We conducted a mixed-methods study combining a scoping review and an exploratory qualitative interview study. The scoping review examined how SDM is described and currently applied in lung cancer screening. Seven databases were searched from inception to February 2025. In parallel, semi-structured interviews were conducted with healthcare professionals (HCPs) (N = 22) and potential screening participants (PSPs) for lung cancer screening (N = 13) in Flanders, Belgium. This represents a pre-implementation context without a population-based lung cancer screening programme altough policy attention is increasing. Interviews explored perceptions of decision-making and expectations regarding SDM. Qualitative data were analysed using the Qualitative Analysis Guide of Leuven (QuAGOL). Thirty-five studies were included in the scoping review. Across studies, SDM in lung cancer screening was associated with improved knowledge, lower decisional conflict, and greater satisfaction, although implementation in real-world clinical practice often remained limited to information provision rather than deliberation. Interview findings complemented the scoping review, demonstrating substantial variation in PSPs' preferred decisional roles. While some PSPs preferred independent decision-making, others valued collaborative decision-making and professional guidance. Across both study components , reassurance, feeling listened to, and personalised support emerged as central aspects of decision-making. HCPs additionally identified important implementation barriers for SDM, including limited consultation time, workload pressures, insufficient training, and challenges related to health literacy and communication. Effective integration of SDM in lung cancer screening requires flexible, adaptive approaches that accommodate diverse decisional needs and avoid procedural or "tick-box" implementation. Embedding SDM within existing care pathways and using complementary professional roles may enhance feasibility. Screening programmes should provide flexible decision support and allow SDM to be distributed across HCPs and encounters, enabling guidance to be tailored to individual preferences and values.
