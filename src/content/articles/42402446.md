### 重點摘要
這項世代研究利用巴西食品與營養監測系統2008-2019年的數據，旨在定義巴西兒童在不同發展階段的飲食模式並評估其轉變，同時探討純母乳餵養與這些模式軌跡之間的關聯。研究發現兒童主要存在兩種飲食模式：高超加工食品攝取和低超加工食品攝取，且這些模式從幼兒期到學齡期呈現顯著穩定性。結果顯示，嬰幼兒時期遵循低超加工食品模式的純母乳餵養兒童，在學齡前有更高的機率維持此健康模式，並降低了轉向高超加工食品模式的可能性。這項研究指出，純母乳餵養可能在保護兒童免於形成高超加工食品飲食模式方面扮演重要角色。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這項研究透過長期追蹤，填補了巴西兒童飲食模式在不同發展階段的動態變化及其穩定性的研究空白，並量化了純母乳餵養對預防兒童轉向高超加工食品飲食模式的保護作用。然而，研究並未深入探討純母乳餵養影響飲食模式轉變的具體生理或行為機制，也未提供針對改變不健康飲食模式的具體干預策略。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Tracking of dietary patterns from early childhood to school age in the Brazilian Food and Nutritional Surveillance System, 2008-2019.
**Abstract:** i) Define children's dietary patterns at three developmental stages-early childhood (1 to <3 years), preschool age (3 to <6 years), and school age (6 to <11 years)-and assess transitions over time; ii) Verify the association between exposure to exclusive breastfeeding and the trajectory of patterns. Cohort study using 2008-2019 data from the Brazilian Food and Nutritional Surveillance System. Standardized markers of previous-day food intake identified dietary patterns. Latent Transition Analysis assessed shifts over time and estimated the effect of exclusive breastfeeding for 3-6 months on dietary pattern transitions. 135,340 children. Two dietary patterns emerged: higher ultra processed food intake and lower ultra processed food intake. Patterns showed notable stability over time. Exclusively breastfed children following a lower ultra processed food pattern in early childhood had 11% higher odds (OR = 1.11, 95% CI = 1.06 - 1.16) of maintaining this pattern at preschool age. Exclusively breastfed children in the lower ultra processed food pattern had a 10% lower likelihood (OR = 0.90, 95% CI = 0.86 - 0.95) of transitioning to a higher ultra processed food pattern at preschool age. Dietary patterns in Brazilian children showed significant stability from early childhood to school age. Exclusive breastfeeding may protect against transitioning to high ultra-processed food patterns.
