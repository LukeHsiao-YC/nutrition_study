### 重點摘要
本研究旨在探討青少年使用TikTok如何影響其飲食攝取，以及父母在此影響中的角色。研究方法是透過對25名15-16歲高中生進行半結構式焦點團體訪談，並進行歸納式主題分析。結果發現，TikTok的食物內容會透過趨勢、廣告、影片呈現、影響者等多種因素激發青少年的食慾，而青少年普遍認為父母不常參與其TikTok使用，但在協助他們脫離平台時能發揮保護作用。研究結論指出，青少年自覺TikTok食物內容可能影響其飲食，且父母的參與可能具有保護性。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
本研究透過質性方法深入探討了TikTok對青少年飲食的潛在影響及父母的角色，填補了對此議題機制的理解空白。然而，其主要限制在於未能對這些影響與父母介入對青少年實際飲食攝取量所造成的具體衝擊進行量化測量，未來的研究應著重於此一面向。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** "I wanna try it so bad": How TikTok Influences Adolescent Eating and the Role of Parents.
**Abstract:** Explore how adolescents' TikTok use influences their dietary intake and the role of parents in that influence. Four semistructured focus groups were conducted with 25 students (aged 15-16 years) from a Midwestern high school. Participants completed demographic and TikTok use surveys. Transcripts were cleaned, independently coded, and analyzed inductively using a 6-step thematic analysis process. Codes and themes were finalized by consensus. Two themes emerged. First, TikTok food content cued adolescents' appetite through subthemes related to content type (e.g., trends, ads), video factors (e.g., food presentation, comments), influencers, and contextual cues. Second, adolescents viewed parents as generally uninvolved in their TikTok use but as protective when they facilitated disengagement with TikTok, interrupting food-related content exposure. Adolescents reported that TikTok food content may influence their eating, and parental involvement may be protective. Future quantitative research is needed to measure the impacts of these influences on dietary intake.
