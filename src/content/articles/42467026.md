### 重點摘要
這項質性研究旨在探討圍產期婦女的身體意象、對非飲食法的障礙及接受度。研究透過八場線上焦點團體訪談了20名孕婦及產後婦女，收集她們對身體意象、自我照護及非飲食法融入生活的看法。分析歸納出五個主題，包括對非飲食法的參與度與開放性、認知障礙、誤解、矛盾心理，以及圍產期身體形象支持來源。研究建議母嬰營養提供者應探討社會壓力、非飲食原則的誤解以及嬰兒照護的實際挑戰。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
本研究透過質性方法深入了解圍產期婦女對非飲食法的複雜看法，包括其誤解、障礙與接受度，為該特定群體的身體意象與飲食行為研究提供了細緻的視角。然而，作為一項質性研究，其結果的推廣性可能受限於有限的樣本量和特定的招募來源。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Postpartum Body Image and Eating Behaviors: Receptivity to Nondiet Approaches in a Qualitative Study of Perinatal Women.
**Abstract:** This study explored body image as well as barriers and receptivity to nondiet approaches among pregnant and postpartum individuals. Eight focus groups were conducted online with 2 facilitators. Data were collected from January through July 2024. Focus groups lasting 60-90 minutes were conducted virtually. Twenty participants were recruited from the Arizona Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants, and Children program, a doula support group, and snowball sampling. Pregnant and postpartum participants provided responses regarding body image, practicing self-care, and receptivity to incorporating nondiet approaches. Downloaded Zoom version 6.0, 2024 audio files were transcribed, followed by thematic analysis. Coding took place iteratively among independent coders. After consensus of the code book, each coder analyzed all the focus groups. Major themes and subthemes were refined and finalized throughout the analysis. Five themes were identified: (1) current engagement in and openness to nondiet approaches, (2) perceived barriers, (3) misinterpretations, (4) ambivalence, and (5) sources of body image support in the perinatal period. Maternal nutrition providers can discuss societal pressures, misinterpretations of nondiet principles, and practical challenges related to infant care throughout the perinatal period.
