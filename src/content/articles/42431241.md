### 重點摘要
這項研究探討了幼兒對食物的信念如何形成食物道德化的認知基礎。研究團隊讓4至6歲的兒童將食物分為「健康」、「喜歡」和「可食」與否，並分析其判斷在不同年齡層的差異以及與父母飲食行為的關聯。結果顯示，雖然兒童對食物的喜好程度相似，但隨著年齡增長，年紀較大的兒童對食物「健康」和「可食」的判斷更趨於一致，且與成人的健康判斷相似，並逐漸將可食性與健康而非喜好連結。這些發現表明，幼兒時期對食物可食性的信念會隨年齡變化，為食物道德化奠定基礎，並指出父母的飲食相關行為亦會影響兒童對食物可食性的概念。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇文獻填補了早期兒童食物道德化認知基礎的研究空白，揭示了社會信念在兒童對食物可食性判斷中的早期內化過程。未來的研究應進一步探討食物道德化的出現機制、長期影響，以及如何透過教育來培養健康的飲食關係，以避免過度道德化帶來的負面後果。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** The cognitive foundations of food moralization.
**Abstract:** By adulthood, we attach moral valence to food-labeling some foods as "good," others as "bad," and judging ourselves and others based on the foods we choose. Here, we examined young children's beliefs about which foods are permissible to eat and whether these conceptions vary across early childhood--shifting patterns of normative alignment that may reflect early internalization of societal beliefs about food and lay a cognitive foundation for food moralization. We presented 4- to 6-year-old children with 12 foods (four healthy, four unhealthy, four of mixed-health status; based on adult health ratings) and asked children to categorize each as "healthy" or "not healthy" (Health); "liked" or "not liked" (Desirability); and "OK" or "not OK" (Permissibility). Although children's Desirability categorizations were similar across ages (with unhealthy foods categorized as "liked" most often), older (vs. younger) children's Health and Permissibility categorizations were more closely aligned with each other and with adult health judgments. Moreover, whereas younger children positively associated Permissibility with Desirability, older children did not. In contrast, a positive association between Permissibility and Health was stronger among older children. Parents' food-related behaviors further predicted differences in how children conceptualized Permissibility. Together, these findings reveal differences across early childhood in beliefs regarding what is permissible to eat--shifting patterns that may lay a foundation on which food moralization is built. The work paves the way for continued research on the emergence and consequences of food moralization and for efforts to teach about health in ways that support healthy relationships with food.
