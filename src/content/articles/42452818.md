### 重點摘要
本研究旨在探討美國1-5歲幼兒含糖飲料（SSB）攝取量與家庭貧困收入比（IPR）的州級關聯，以了解社會經濟和地理因素的複合影響。透過分析2021-2023年國家兒童健康調查的55,399名兒童數據，發現全國有21.1%的幼兒每週攝取SSB至少四次，且低收入家庭（IPR <130%）的攝取率顯著高於高收入家庭（33.1% vs. 12.1%）。研究亦揭示SSB攝取存在顯著的州際差異，介於8.6%至33.7%之間，且在大多數州中，較低的IPR與較高的SSB攝取量相關，但關聯強度因州而異。這些發現強調了為降低幼兒SSB攝取量，需針對低收入家庭的需求制定客製化的介入措施。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇文獻填補了以往對家庭收入和地理區域如何共同影響幼兒含糖飲料攝取量知識的空白，特別是透過州級分析揭示了收入對攝取量的影響在不同州之間存在差異。本研究的限制在於其橫斷面設計無法建立因果關係，且未能深入探究造成各州差異的具體原因。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** State-Level Differences in Sugar-Sweetened Beverage Consumption by Income Among Children Aged 1-5 Years, United States, 2021-2023.
**Abstract:** Sugar-sweetened beverage (SSB) consumption among young children can be associated with obesity and other adverse health outcomes. Consumption varies by socioeconomic factors such as household income and geographic region, although how these factors might jointly affect SSB consumption is unknown. We evaluated state-level associations between income-to-poverty ratio (IPR) and SSB consumption among children aged 1-5 years in the United States. We conducted a pooled cross-sectional study of 55,399 children aged 1-5 years using 2021-2023 National Survey of Children's Health data. We estimated the prevalence of SSB consumption ≥4 times per week by household IPR (<130%, 130%-349%, ≥350%) and state. Multivariable logistic regression evaluated the association between IPR and SSB consumption after adjusting for age, sex, and race and ethnicity. Nationally, 21.1% (95% confidence interval [CI]: 20.2%-21.9%) of children consumed SSBs ≥4 times per week, and this varied significantly by IPR (<130%: 33.1% [95% CI: 30.7%-35.6%], 130%-349%: 22.4% [95% CI: 21.0%-23.9%], ≥350%: 12.1% [95% CI: 11.2%-13.0%]). Substantial geographic variation was observed; SSB consumption ranged from 8.6% (95% CI: 6.4%-11.3%) in Vermont to 33.7% (95% CI: 28.9%-38.8%) in Mississippi. In multivariable models, children from households with a lower IPR (<130% compared with ≥350%) had significantly higher SSB consumption for most states, although the magnitude of this association differed by state. To reduce SSB consumption among young children, findings highlight the need for potential interventions tailored to the needs of those residing in low-income households.
