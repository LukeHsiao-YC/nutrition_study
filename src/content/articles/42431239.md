### 重點摘要
這項隨機對照試驗旨在評估強化生活型態介入（包含熱量-碳水化合物限制飲食或限時飲食結合熱量-碳水化合物限制）對第二型糖尿病患者食慾和暴食行為的影響。研究將120名超重或肥胖的第二型糖尿病成人分為熱量-碳水化合物限制組、限時飲食結合熱量-碳水化合物限制組或對照組，並提供運動及行為教育。結果顯示，兩種介入組在12週和24週時均顯著改善了主觀食慾感知（降低飢餓感、進食慾望，增加飽足感），但各組間的暴食量表分數和客觀暴食發作次數並無顯著差異。總體而言，強化生活型態介入能有效降低第二型糖尿病成人的食慾，但對暴食行為沒有顯著影響。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
本研究填補了強化生活型態介入對第二型糖尿病患者食慾與暴食行為影響的知識空白，證實了其在食慾管理方面的有效性。然而，由於研究未能觀察到對暴食行為的顯著影響，未來的研究應針對基線暴食量表分數較高的人群進行，以更明確地評估此類介入對暴食行為的潛在益處。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Effects of intensive lifestyle interventions with calorie-carbohydrate-restricted diet versus time-restricted eating on appetite and binge eating in type 2 diabetes: A randomized controlled trial.
**Abstract:** The impact of intensive lifestyle interventions on appetite regulation and binge eating in individuals with type 2 diabetes (T2D) remains unclear. This study evaluated the effects of combined lifestyle interventions on appetite responses and binge eating in overweight or obese adults with T2D. In a randomized trial, 120 participants with T2D were allocated to three groups (n = 40 each): (1) Calorie-carbohydrate restriction (CCR), (2) Time-restricted eating with CCR (TRE + CCR), or (3) Control. Intervention groups received structured exercise and behavioral education based on the Information-Motivation-Behavioral Skills model. Appetite perceptions (hunger, satiety, desire to eat, and prospective food consumption) and binge eating (Binge Eating Scale; BES and objective binge episodes) were evaluated at baseline, week 12, and week 24 using linear mixed models. Both CCR and TRE + CCR significantly improved subjective appetite compared with the control group at 12 and 24 weeks (all p < 0.01). At 24 weeks, hunger decreased by -24.1 mm (95% CI: -35.8, -12.5) in the CCR group and -32.7 mm (95% CI: -44.2, -21.3) in the TRE + CCR group. Satiety also increased by 21.7 mm (95% CI: 9.46, 33.9) and 29.4 mm (95% CI: 17.4, 41.4), respectively. Significant reductions were observed for desire to eat and prospective food consumption. In contrast, changes in BES and objective binge episodes were not significantly different between groups at any time point. No significant differences were detected between the CCR and the TRE + CCR groups. Intensive lifestyle interventions incorporating CCR or TRE + CCR effectively reduced appetite in adults with T2D but did not significantly affect binge eating. Future research should target individuals with higher baseline BES scores to clarify potential benefits for binge eating behavior.
