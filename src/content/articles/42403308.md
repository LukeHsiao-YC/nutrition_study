### 重點摘要
該研究旨在探討在食用含麩質飲食的潛在乳糜瀉 (PCD) 兒童的長期結果，PCD 定義為血清學陽性但無絨毛萎縮。這項多中心縱向研究追蹤了 88 名兒童 PCD 患者，平均追蹤時間為 3.3 年。結果顯示，約五分之一的患者進展為乳糜瀉，同時超過三分之一的患者在維持含麩質飲食下實現了抗組織轉穀氨醯胺抗體 (tTG) 的血清學正常化。研究結論指出，基線血清學水平不能可靠預測黏膜惡化，PCD 的異質性結果需定期進行血清學和組織學追蹤。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這項研究提供了兒童潛在乳糜瀉患者在持續攝取含麩質飲食下的長期追蹤數據，特別是關於疾病進展及血清學正常化的異質性結果，填補了此群體自然病程預後預測指標不足的研究空白。未來研究應著重於尋找更可靠的生物標誌物來預測黏膜惡化風險。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Heterogeneous outcomes in pediatric potential celiac disease: A multicenter longitudinal study.
**Abstract:** Potential celiac disease (PCD) is defined by positive celiac serology without villous atrophy. We aimed to study the outcome of children with PCD consuming a gluten-containing diet (GCD) over time. Children diagnosed with PCD between December 2018 and January 2022, in three pediatric gastroenterology centers, were longitudinally followed. Data were collected until October 2024, with a minimum follow-up of 12 months, unless celiac disease (CeD) was diagnosed earlier. Baseline data included demographics, celiac serology, and histological findings. Follow-up data included serology and biopsy results when performed. The cohort included 88 patients with PCD, with a mean follow-up of 39 (range 12- 69) months. During this period, 17 (19.5%) patients progressed to CeD. Normalization of anti-tissue transglutaminase antibodies (tTG), while continuing a GCD, occurred in 33/88 (37.5%) while the rest remained with PCD. Specifically, among 16 patients with tTG levels >×10 upper limit of normal (ULN) at PCD diagnosis, three (18.8%) progressed to CeD, six (37.5%) normalized tTG while continued GCD, and the rest remained unchanged. Asymptomatic patients had higher rates of tTG normalization (61.9% vs. 35.8%, p = 0.0011). Neither tTG levels, nor having Marsh 1 histology, had a significant effect on PCD outcomes. Among pediatric patients with PCD, during a mean follow-up of 3.3 years, one-fifth progressed to CeD. Baseline serology levels do not reliably predict future mucosal deterioration. Over a third of children with PCD may achieve serological normalization while maintaining a GCD. This heterogeneous pattern of PCD warrants periodic serological and histological follow-up.
