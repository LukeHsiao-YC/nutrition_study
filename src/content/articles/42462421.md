### 重點摘要
這項全國性數據連結研究旨在探討精神疾病患者住院期間被診斷出營養不良的發生率及其相關住院時間，並與非精神疾病患者進行比較。研究發現，精神疾病患者在住院期間被診斷為營養不良的發生率顯著更高（每10萬人年3526次），且住院天數更長，即使排除飲食失調或有機精神疾病等因素，此風險仍高達非精神疾病患者的7.76倍與11.44倍。研究結論指出，無論住院原因為何，精神疾病患者在住院期間被發現營養不良的風險都較高，因此急需針對此族群制定預防和早期識別策略。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
本研究填補了精神疾病患者營養不良發生率及其對住院時間影響的數據空白，明確指出此族群的風險顯著高於一般人群，且此現象獨立於飲食失調或特定精神科住院原因。這凸顯了過去對此重要公共衛生議題關注不足的現狀。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Rates of malnutrition identified during hospital admissions and related length of stay for people with mental illness: A population-wide data linkage study.
**Abstract:** People with mental illness experience numerous risk factors for malnutrition, however, the rate and impact of malnutrition in this population group have not been examined. We aimed to determine the rates of malnutrition identified during hospital admissions and the associated length-of-stay in mental health service users compared to non-mental health service users. In a population-wide data linkage study, malnutrition diagnoses made during hospital admissions and associated bed-days were examined for mental health service users compared to non-mental health service users between 2017 and 2019. Primary and secondary outcomes were crude and standardized rates of malnutrition and associated bed-days, respectively, presented as incidence and days per 100,000 person years. Sensitivity analyses assessed the contributions of: (i) eating disorder diagnoses, (ii) organic mental disorders, and (iii) admissions for mental health care. Mental health service users were admitted with malnutrition 3526 times per 100,000 person years. This rate was higher than for non-mental health service users, who experienced 593 admissions per 100, 000 person-years. In the fully adjusted model (aIRR), mental health service users had 7.76x (95% CI 7.74-7.77) higher rates of malnutrition being identified during an admission and 11.44x (95% CI 11.43-11.44) higher bed-days for those admissions compared to non-mental health service users. Excluding people with an eating disorder diagnosis had little effect on incidence of malnutrition (aIRR = 7.35, 95% CI 7.34-7.37) or bed-days (aIRR = 10.80, 95% CI 10.79-10.80). Excluding hospitalizations for organic mental disorders produced similar estimates (malnutrition aIRR = 7.65, 95% CI 7.63-7.67; bed-days aIRR = 11.24, 95% CI 11.23-11.24). Excluding malnutrition diagnosed during a non-organic mental health care admission reduced the incidence rates of malnutrition diagnoses being identified during hospital admissions (aIRR = 6.33, 95% CI 6.31-6.35) and bed-days (aIRR = 8.08, 95% CI 8.07-8.08), though rates remained significantly higher compared to non-mental health service users. Mental health service users are at higher risk for malnutrition being identified during a hospital admission, irrespective of whether the admission was for an episode of mental or physical health care. Strategies for prevention and early-identification are needed for this population group.
