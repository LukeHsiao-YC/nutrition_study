### 重點摘要
本研究旨在探討加護病房(ICU)存活者在住院恢復期間的食慾、嗅覺和味覺感知與營養攝取達標的關聯性。研究比較了50名ICU存活者與50名一般病房患者，發現兩組在入院時及追蹤期間的食慾、嗅覺和味覺感知並無差異，儘管ICU存活者的這些感知在六週內有顯著改善。然而，ICU存活者的口服能量和蛋白質攝取量顯著低於一般病房患者。研究結論為，ICU存活者雖然感知上沒有明顯的食慾或感官問題，但其營養攝取不足的真正障礙仍需進一步研究。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這項研究發現ICU存活者與一般病房患者在食慾、嗅覺和味覺感知上沒有差異，但ICU存活者的口服能量和蛋白質攝取量卻顯著較低。這填補了ICU後患者營養攝取不足是否直接源於食慾或感官知覺下降的疑問，但同時也凸顯了新的研究空白：即導致ICU存活者營養攝取不足的真正障礙並非單純來自食慾或感官知覺的下降，未來的研究應著重於探討這些尚不明確的潛在因素。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Associations between ICU survival and self-perceived appetite, smell, and taste relative to general ward patients: A prospective study.
**Abstract:** Post-intensive care unit (ICU) patients frequently fail to meet nutritional requirements during recovery on the hospital ward. Alterations in appetite, smell and taste perception in ICU survivors remain poorly understood. This study compared appetite and sensory perceptions between ICU survivors and general ward patients and assessed relationships with nutritional goal achievement during hospitalization and early recovery. In this single-center, observational prospective cohort study, 50 post-ICU patients (ICU stay ≥48 h) and 50 acutely admitted general ward patients were enrolled (June 2023-February 2024). Appetite, taste and smell perception were the primary outcome parameters and were assessed within 24 h of ward admission, after 7 days or at discharge, and at 6 weeks using visual analog scales. Multivariable linear regression and mixed-effect models analyzed associations between post-ICU status and outcome parameters at ward admission and during follow-up. No differences in appetite, taste, or smell were observed between post-ICU and general ward patients at ward admission or during follow-up (all P > 0.05). Post-ICU showed significant improvements in appetite, taste, and smell within 6 weeks (P < 0.001, P = 0.01, and P = 0.02, respectively), in contrast to general ward patients (all P > 0.05). Post-ICU patients achieved lower energy (48% ± 28% vs 72% ± 33%, P = 0.00) and protein adequacy (46% ± 29% vs 72% ± 39%, P = 0.00) versus general ward patients. We observed no differences in appetite, smell, or taste between post-ICU and general ward patients at ward admission or during recovery. Post-ICU achieved lower energy and protein adequacy by oral intake but barriers of reduced intake remain to be further investigated.
