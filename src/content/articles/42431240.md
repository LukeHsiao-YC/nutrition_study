### 重點摘要
本研究旨在探討荷蘭8歲兒童居住地周圍的食物環境與飲食結果之間的關聯，並評估母親教育程度（代表家庭社經地位）的調節作用。研究利用地理資訊系統評估快餐店密度和食物環境健康分數，並透過家長問卷收集飲食數據。結果顯示，食物環境與兒童飲食的關聯有限且出乎意料，例如較健康的食物環境分數反而與較差的飲食品質相關，而較多的速食店暴露在母親教育程度較高的情況下卻與較好的飲食品質相關。研究結論指出，在都市環境中，鄰里食物環境與兒童飲食的關聯性較弱且不一致，強調未來研究需考量家庭資源和文化背景。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇研究探討了在特定城市文化（荷蘭鹿特丹）中，鄰里食物環境對兒童飲食的影響，填補了此領域在非美國背景下證據不一的空白。然而，研究結果的有限性和不一致性，特別是反常的關聯性，強調了未來研究和介入策略需要更全面地考量家庭資源、文化背景及多面向因素，暗示單純改變環境可能不足以有效支持兒童健康飲食。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Associations between the residential food environment and diet: The Generation R Study.
**Abstract:** The neighbourhood food environment is a potentially modifiable determinant of children's diets, yet evidence for associations with dietary outcomes remains mixed. This study examined cross-sectional associations between residential food environment characteristics and dietary outcomes among 8-year-old Dutch children, and whether maternal education (indicating household socioeconomic status) moderated these associations. Data were drawn from (n = 3739) children in the Generation R Study, a population-based birth cohort in Rotterdam, the Netherlands. Residential food environment exposure was assessed using Geographic Information System-based measures of absolute and relative fast-food outlet density and an overall neighbourhood food environment healthiness score within 400m from children's residences. Dietary outcomes were derived from a parent-reported Food Frequency Questionnaire, and included a validated overall diet quality score (out of 10), and non-core food and drink intake (g/day). Associations were examined using generalized linear models, adjusting for potential confounders. Findings revealed limited and unexpected associations. A healthier food environment score was associated with children's poorer diet quality, and no interaction with maternal education was found. Greater absolute fast-food outlet exposure was associated with better diet quality, but only among children with higher-educated mothers (+0.021 point/outlet, 95% CI: 0.008, 0.034). No associations were found between relative fast-food outlet density and any dietary outcomes. No consistent associations were found for food environment exposure and non-core food and drink intake. In the urban Dutch context, neighbourhood food environments showed weak and inconsistent associations with child diet, with some evidence of potential moderation by household socioeconomic status. These findings highlight the need for multidimensional research and intervention strategies that consider family resources and cultural context to effectively support healthy eating among children.
