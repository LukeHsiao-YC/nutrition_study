### 重點摘要
這項研究旨在描述美國和日本BMI升高者中，是否有肥胖診斷的患者特徵及其隨時間的趨勢。研究分析了美國和日本數百萬BMI≥30 kg/m²的成年人數據，發現美國有70.1%的此類人群獲得肥胖診斷，而日本僅有2.3%。結果顯示，在兩國中，有肥胖診斷的患者其相關疾病（如第二型糖尿病、慢性腎病）的盛行率顯著高於沒有診斷的患者。研究結論指出，在BMI升高的人群中存在肥胖診斷不足的問題，且相關疾病的盛行率很高。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇文獻明確指出在美國和日本的BMI升高人群中普遍存在肥胖診斷不足的現象，並量化了此現象與多種相關疾病盛行率之間的關係，從而填補了對肥胖診斷現狀及其與共病關聯理解的空白。然而，研究並未探討造成肥胖診斷差異的原因（尤其美國與日本之間巨大的診斷率差異），也未提供如何改善診斷率或早期介入以減輕疾病負擔的具體策略，這將是未來研究可以深入的方向。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Trends in Diagnosing Obesity: The CALOR Study.
**Abstract:** This study aimed to describe characteristics of people with elevated BMI by presence versus absence of obesity diagnosis and trends over time. Data from adults with recorded BMI ≥ 30 kg/m In the United States (N = 3,584,120; 70.1% with obesity diagnosis), prevalence of interrelated diseases was high overall and higher in those with versus without obesity diagnosis, e.g., type 2 diabetes (49.3% vs. 29.9%), chronic kidney disease (32.9% vs. 18.8%), and heart failure (23.3% vs. 9.8%). In Japan (N = 3,140,900; 2.3% with obesity diagnosis), interrelated disease burden was also higher in those with versus without obesity diagnosis, e.g., type 2 diabetes (42.2% vs. 25.9%) and chronic kidney disease (14.3% vs. 8.6%). In the United States, interrelated disease burden was generally higher for those first diagnosed in 2024 versus those diagnosed in 2016-2020. In people with elevated BMI in the United States and Japan, there was underdiagnosis of obesity and a high prevalence of interrelated diseases.
