### 重點摘要
鑑於基於腸泌素的藥物在肥胖治療上存在限制，本研究旨在探討自然策略在食慾抑制方面的作用。透過對主要生物醫學資料庫的系統性回顧，整合了自然策略的機制和臨床證據。結果顯示這些策略具有生物學合理性，但人體證據零碎、異質且效果通常小而短期，與客觀攝取量和長期臨床結果的連結不一致。雖然自然策略可作為輔助方法，但目前證據支持謹慎整合而非單獨使用，且需要更嚴謹的未來研究。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
本文填補了對自然食慾抑制策略的機制與臨床證據的整合性回顧，指出了其作為肥胖管理輔助手段的潛力。然而，研究限制在於現有人體證據零碎且異質，缺乏標準化的食慾測量、客觀攝取評估、長期追蹤以及對安全性與產品品質的嚴謹報告，這些都是未來研究需克服的挑戰。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Natural Approaches to Appetite Suppression in Obesity: Mechanisms, Evidence, and Clinical Integration.
**Abstract:** The rapid clinical uptake of incretin-based antiobesity pharmacotherapies has reinforced appetite regulation as a core therapeutic target. However, real-world implementation is often constrained by cost, access, tolerability, and patient preferences, sustaining demand for complementary, lower-cost strategies that may support appetite control and adherence to lifestyle interventions. This review synthesizes mechanistic and clinical evidence on natural strategies intended to suppress appetite as adjuncts to obesity management, integrating homeostatic and hedonic appetite regulation with gut-brain signaling. A structured search of major biomedical databases from inception to June 2025 was conducted, and evidence was synthesized qualitatively by intervention type. Mechanistic and preclinical findings were used to contextualize human data. Across natural strategies, biologic plausibility is common, but the human evidence base remains fragmented and heterogeneous in intervention definition, outcome measurement, and follow-up duration. Reported effects are generally small to modest and short term, with inconsistent links to objective intake measures and durable clinical outcomes. Safety and tolerability profiles vary across approaches, and product or formulation variability, limited long-term data, and inconsistent quality reporting frequently constrain interpretation and generalizability. Overall, natural strategies for appetite suppression may be considered biologically plausible adjuncts, but current evidence supports cautious integration rather than stand-alone use. Future studies should prioritize standardized appetite measurement, objective intake assessments, longer follow-up, and rigorous reporting of safety, tolerability, and product quality to enable clinically actionable guidance.
