### 重點摘要
本研究旨在理解因心臟衰竭住院之老年患者對住院照護的目標，並透過對24名患者進行質性訪談，運用「患者優先照護 (PPC)」框架來識別其目標。研究結果顯示，患者的住院目標主要為「感覺好轉並回家」，然而其更廣泛的健康結果目標則聚焦於獨立性、社會連結與維持日常作息。患者普遍認為住院照護僅提供短期症狀緩解，與其長期的健康優先事項脫節。因此，研究建議臨床醫師應連結短期治療目標與患者的廣泛價值和實際健康目標，並進行預後與權衡的明確討論。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這項研究填補了理解因心臟衰竭住院之老年患者，在短期住院治療目標與其更廣泛的長期健康優先事項之間存在認知脫節的研究空白。未來的研究可進一步探索有效的臨床溝通策略，以協助醫病之間更好地整合這些不同的目標，並指導共享決策。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** A qualitative study of goals of hospital care and health outcome goals among patients admitted for heart failure.
**Abstract:** We sought to understand how hospitalized older adults describe their goals for hospital care. Eliciting patient healthcare goals is the first domain of the Center for Medicare and Medicaid Services' Age Friendly Hospital measure. Heart failure, a leading cause of hospitalization and readmission in older adults, is an ideal condition for exploring how hospitalized patients articulate goals. We conducted qualitative interviews with 24 patients admitted for heart failure, using the Patient Priorities Care (PPC) framework to identify their goals for hospitalization and health outcome goals. PPC is a communication intervention that identifies patients' values and health outcome goals to guide shared decision-making. Patients' goals for hospitalization were clear and uniform: to feel better and go home. Values and health outcome goals centered on independence, social connection, and maintaining routines, which patients experienced as disconnected from recurrent hospitalizations that provided only short-term symptom relief. Patients articulated goals of hospitalization related to short-term symptom relief, but felt that over the longer term, hospital care was disconnected from their broader health priorities. When eliciting goals for hospitalization, clinicians should connect immediate treatment goals with patients' broader values and realistic health outcome goals through explicit discussion of prognosis and tradeoffs.
