### 重點摘要
本研究旨在探討第二型糖尿病中高血糖和高血脂如何影響骨骼肌中關鍵調節因子nNOS的表達。透過db/db小鼠模型和C2C12肌管細胞實驗，研究發現db/db小鼠存在高血脂、肌肉量減少和nNOS蛋白表達降低。體外實驗進一步揭示，高脂肪酸（特別是棕櫚酸）而非高血糖，是導致nNOS表達下降和肌管融合受損的主要原因。因此，研究結論指出脂質過載可能透過nNOS訊號受損連結第二型糖尿病的肌肉萎縮，並提供潛在的治療靶點。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
此研究填補了第二型糖尿病患者骨骼肌中nNOS失調機制不明的知識空白，明確指出高血脂（脂質過載）而非高血糖，是導致nNOS表達下降和肌肉萎縮的關鍵驅動因素。本研究未直接提及未來的研究限制，但其成果為未來針對nNOS作為治療靶點以維持骨骼肌質量的研究奠定了基礎。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Neuronal nitirc oxide synthase (nNOS) expression is reduced in skeletal muscle from db/db mice and glucose and fatty acids show differential effects on nNOS regulation and myogenic differentiation.
**Abstract:** Neuronal nitric oxide synthase (nNOS, encoded by NOS1) is a key regulator of skeletal muscle mass. However, the mechanisms underlying nNOS dysregulation in diabetic skeletal muscle remain unclear. We examined the relative contributions of hyperglycemia and hyperlipidemia, hallmarks of type 2 diabetes (T2D), to the regulation of nNOS in a mouse model and C2C12 myotubes. Glucose tolerance, blood parameters, muscle and fat mass, Nos1 DNA methylation levels, mRNA/protein expression were evaluated in male db/db and C57BL/6J wild-type control mice (n = 4/group, 12 weeks old). C2C12 myotubes were treated with glucose (5, 25, or 60 mM) or fatty acids (0.05-0.75 mM), including palmitic acid (PA) and oleic acid (OA). Fusion index and mRNA/protein expression were evaluated. All data were analyzed using Student's t-test or ANOVA. db/db mice exhibited impaired glucose tolerance (3-fold higher, P < 0.05), hyperlipidemia (triglyceride levels, 2-fold higher, P < 0.01), and reduced muscle mass in plantaris muscle (46% lower, P < 0.01). nNOS protein expression decreased in plantaris muscle (29% lower, P < 0.01) and was positively correlated with muscle mass (r = 0.87, P < 0.01). Nos1μ expression was reduced in diabetic plantaris muscle (64% lower, P < 0.01), whereas Nos1 DNA methylation levels were unchanged. Among the nutritional factors tested, 0.75 mM PA, but not high glucose, reduced nNOS expression (41% lower, P < 0.05) and impaired myotube fusion in C2C12 myotubes (61% lower, P < 0.01). However, OA exhibited no detrimental effects. Importantly, high glucose did not downregulate nNOS expression. The combination treatment demonstrated that PA, but not glucose, was the primary determinant of nNOS downregulation (70% lower, P < 0.01). Impaired nNOS signaling may link lipid overload, rather than hyperglycemia, to muscle atrophy in T2D and represents a potential therapeutic target for preserving skeletal muscle mass.
