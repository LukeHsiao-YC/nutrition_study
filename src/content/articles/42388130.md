### 重點摘要
這篇混和方法系統性文獻回顧旨在彙整成人對膳食脂肪攝取相關的健康價值與偏好，透過檢索多個資料庫至2025年5月。研究納入了11項量性與2項質性研究，儘管旨在涵蓋所有國家，但主要研究來自高收入地區。結果顯示，人們對脂肪的看法複雜且常相互矛盾，普遍將高脂肪食物視為不健康但認為植物油有益，並表現出減少脂肪攝取的意願。然而，這些主題的證據確定性從非常低到中等，且會因性別、年齡和飲食模式而異。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
本研究透過系統性整合現有文獻，填補了對成人膳食脂肪攝取相關健康價值與偏好認知上的空白。然而，由於證據確定性普遍偏低，且大部分納入的研究來自高收入國家，未來仍需更多高品質的研究，特別是來自不同經濟背景地區的研究，以強化證據基礎並為膳食指南提供更精準的建議。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Adults' health-related values and preferences related to reducing or modifying dietary fat intake: a mixed methods systematic review.
**Abstract:** To systematically summarise people's health-related values and preferences related to fat intake. The search through MEDLINE, EMBASE, Web of Science, SSCI, CAB, AGRIS, and FSTA up to May 2025 was performed. Qualitative and quantitative studies were included. Screening of abstracts and full-text articles, extraction and risk of bias assessment were performed by two independent reviewers. Data was summarised narratively, and the certainty of evidence was assessed using the GRADE methodology. Quantitative and qualitative evidence was analysed using a convergent integrated approach. This review aimed to include studies from all types of countries; however, most included studies were conducted in high-income settings. Primary studies of adults (aged ≥18 years), with or without cardiometabolic conditions were included. From 52,166 screened records, we included 11 quantitative and 2 qualitative studies; studies were primarily conducted in high-income countries. Five main themes were identified: i) negative perception of fat in food, ii) positive perception of vegetable oil as being beneficial to health, iii) willingness to lower fat consumption, iv) willingness to pay for healthier fat content, and v) barriers towards unsaturated fat consumption. The most frequently reported themes were negative perception of fat and willingness to consume low-fat products, while prioritizing vegetable oil as a healthy fat. The certainty of the evidence for these themes was very low to moderate, rated down for risk of bias and indirectness issues. The perception of fats and oils in diets is complex and often contradictory, with most people viewing high-fat products as unhealthy and associating them with weight gain, while oils are generally perceived to be beneficial. However, these views can vary significantly depending on gender, age, and dietary patterns. Overall, the evidence supporting these perceptions is of moderate to very low certainty, and to inform guideline recommendations more research is need.
