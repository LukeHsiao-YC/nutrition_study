### 重點摘要
本研究旨在探討全食物干預對社區居住老年人蛋白質攝取量的有效性，並識別成功的干預特徵，以作為蛋白質補充劑外更具永續性的替代方案。透過對16項研究的系統性回顧與11項研究的統合分析，發現全食物干預能顯著增加老年人的每日蛋白質攝取量，平均提升12.2克/天。研究結果指出，長期、個別諮詢、面對面或混合式交付，以及明確蛋白質目標的干預措施，是提升蛋白質攝取效果的關鍵。總結來說，全食物干預是提高老年人蛋白質攝取的有效策略，且其永續性優於短期蛋白質補充。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
這篇文獻填補了在老年族群中，全食物飲食干預作為一種可持續提升蛋白質攝取策略的有效性及其關鍵特徵的研究空白，提供了蛋白質補充劑以外的實證替代方案。文獻著重於識別使全食物干預成功的具體實施細節，對於臨床實踐和公共衛生政策制定具有重要指導意義。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Effectiveness of Whole Food Interventions on Protein Intake in Community-Dwelling Older Adults: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis.
**Abstract:** Adequate dietary protein intake contributes to preservation of muscle mass, muscle strength, and healthy aging. Protein supplementation is an effective strategy for increasing protein intake, but its effects are often temporary. Alternatively, whole food dietary modifications can sustainably enhance protein consumption. This systematic review and meta-analysis investigated the effectiveness of whole food interventions on protein intake in older adults and explored which characteristics contributed to its effectiveness. A systematic search was conducted on PubMed, Embase, and the Web of Science to identify studies among community-dwelling older adults (aged ≥50 years) that assessed the effect of whole food interventions (≥12 weeks) on daily protein intake (g/day or g/kg/day). The revised Cochrane risk-of-bias tool for randomized trials was used to assess bias. The primary outcome was protein intake. Trials with a control group were included in a random-effects meta-analysis. We included 16 studies (n = 1712 participants, 33% male) in our systematic review. Intervention characteristics associated with success were longer duration (>3 months and up to 6 months), individual counseling sessions, in-person or hybrid delivery, and an absolute protein target (g/day or g/meal). Our meta-analysis (11 studies, n = 1207) revealed a significantly greater increase in daily protein intake (+12.2 g/day, 95% CI: +5.0 to +19.4) and relative protein intake (+0.13 g/kg/day, 95% CI: 0.0 to +0.3) in the whole food intervention group compared with the control group. Whole food interventions can effectively increase protein intake in older adults. Interventions with a longer duration, individual counseling, in-person or hybrid delivery, and absolute protein targets reported greater effects. PROSPERO registration No. CRD42023491283.
