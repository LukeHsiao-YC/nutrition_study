### 重點摘要
鑒於美國第二型糖尿病盛行率持續上升，本研究旨在透過範圍界定回顧，識別並描述以飲食和活動行為為目標、符合美國糖尿病協會建議的有效行為干預措施。研究檢索了多個資料庫的隨機對照試驗，納入23項證明在飲食或活動方面具統計學顯著效果的干預措施，並評估其傳遞模式、目標人群和行為改變技術。結果發現，大多數有效的干預措施主要針對受過大學教育的31-50歲非西班牙裔白人女性，且常採用個人化、數位化或組合模式傳遞，並廣泛運用目標設定、行為指導和社會支持等行為改變技術。儘管數位和組合傳遞方法普遍有效且常用的行為改變技術已被識別，但不同族群的代表性有限，未來研究需評估這些策略對更廣泛人口群體的適用性和有效性。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
本文獻回顧識別出有效的行為干預措施及其特點，填補了美國預防第二型糖尿病行為干預策略的實證知識空白。然而，研究結果主要來自特定人口群體（女性、非西班牙裔白人、受過大學教育），對於其他多元族群的代表性不足，限制了這些策略在更廣泛人口中的普遍適用性，這也成為未來研究的重點限制。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Evidence-Based Behavioral Interventions for Diabetes Prevention in the United States: A Scoping Review Using 24-Hour Recalls and Objective Activity Measures.
**Abstract:** The prevalence of type 2 diabetes (T2D) continues to rise, with behavioral interventions recognized as effective preventive strategies among adults in the USA. The American Diabetes Association (ADA) recommends improving diet, and activity behaviors (physical activity and sedentary behavior) as key targets for T2D prevention. Thus, this scoping review aimed to identify and describe key features of behavioral interventions that successfully improved diet and/or activity behaviors (aligned with ADA), including their delivery modes, target populations, and behavior change techniques (BCTs) used. PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, CINAHL, and EMBASE were searched till February 7, 2026, for randomized controlled trials (RCTs) evaluating efficacious behavioral interventions targeting diet and/or activity. Efficacious interventions were defined as those that demonstrated statistically significant effects on at least diet or activity outcome, assessed with validated measures, such as 24-hr dietary recalls and objective activity assessments, among adults. Data were extracted on study samples, target populations, delivery modes, and BCTs, which were classified using Michie's taxonomy. Twenty-three trials encompassing 2,583 adults were included. Most efficacious US-based interventions were tested among women, non-Hispanic White adults with at least some college education, aged 31-50, in an individual-based format (71%). According to delivery mode, intervention types were grouped into face-to-face (8%), e-interventions (29%), environmental (8%) and combined modes (54%). Common BCTs included goal setting (96%), instruction on how to perform a behavior (83%), social support (79%), feedback on behavior (67%). Although digital and combined delivery approaches were common among efficacious interventions and several frequently used BCTs were identified, the limited representation of diverse populations highlights the need for future research to evaluate the applicability and effectiveness of these strategies across broader demographic groups.
