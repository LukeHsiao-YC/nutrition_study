### 重點摘要
醫學界對於適度飲酒的健康影響長期存在爭議，儘管觀察性研究曾暗示潛在益處，但受限於混淆因子和選擇偏差。為填補此證據空白，歐洲研究理事會資助了UNATI這項大型、務實的隨機對照試驗。該試驗將在西班牙招募逾萬名50至75歲的成年人，隨機分配至完全戒酒或遵循地中海模式的適度飲酒組，進行為期四年的介入。此研究旨在提供關於適度飲酒與健康之間因果關係的高品質證據，以解決長達數十年的爭議。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
本研究旨在填補過去缺乏直接比較戒酒與適度飲酒對長期健康影響的隨機對照試驗的空白。過往的觀察性研究存在混淆因子和選擇偏差的限制，無法確立因果關係，而UNATI試驗將提供高質量的因果證據，以解決這一重要公共衛生議題。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** A large randomized trial assessing alcohol cessation versus moderation on clinical events: UNATI.
**Abstract:** The health effects of moderate alcohol consumption remain a highly debated topic in public health and epidemiology. While observational studies have suggested a possible protective effect of moderate drinking, particularly within Mediterranean patterns, concerns about confounding and selection bias persist. No randomized trial to date has definitively addressed whether alcohol cessation offers superior health benefits compared to sustained moderate drinking. In response to this evidence gap, the European Research Council has funded the University of Navarra Alumni Trialist Initiative (UNATI), a large, pragmatic, randomized controlled trial designed to assess the long-term effects of two behavioral interventions: complete alcohol cessation versus moderated consumption following a Mediterranean pattern. UNATI will enroll over 10,000 adults in Spain (men aged 50-70 and women aged 55-75) who currently consume alcohol regularly. Participants will be randomly assigned to one of the two interventions for four years, with annual clinical evaluations and lifestyle assessments. The trial's hybrid design-combining centralized coordination with a network of regional medical professionals-offers a unique opportunity to generate high-quality causal evidence in a real-world setting. If successful, UNATI could become a landmark study, helping to resolve a decades-long controversy regarding moderate alcohol use and health.
