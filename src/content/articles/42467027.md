### 重點摘要
本研究旨在探討國際成人樣本中，自我評估營養知識、線上營養資訊搜尋與飲食失調行為之間的關聯。透過2021年對來自五個國家逾21,000名成年人的線上問卷橫斷面數據分析，發現較高的自我評估營養知識與暴食、對瘦身過度關注及催吐的發生率增加相關。此外，線上搜尋營養資訊與不同國家的暴食、催吐和對瘦身過度關注有著多樣化的關聯性，結果指出飲食失調與自我評估營養知識及線上資訊來源之間存在橫斷面關聯。

### 研究縫隙 (Research Gap)
本研究填補了在國際成人樣本中，對自我評估營養知識、線上營養資訊搜尋行為與飲食失調之間複雜關聯性的大規模實證數據空缺。然而，其橫斷面研究設計無法確立因果關係，未來的研究應考慮採用縱向研究設計以深入探討這些變量之間的因果鏈結，並進一步指導公共衛生營養研究與教育的發展。

### 原文資訊 (Original English)
**Title:** Disordered Eating, Self-Reported Nutrition Knowledge, and Online Nutrition Information Seeking Among an International Sample of Adults.
**Abstract:** Examine relationships between self-reported nutrition knowledge, seeking nutrition information online, and disordered eating. Cross-sectional data were drawn from online surveys in 2021 among adults from Australia, Canada, Mexico, the United Kingdom, and the United States (N = 21,138). Adjusted logistic models generated odds ratios to assess relationships between self-reported nutrition knowledge (ordinal) and online sources of nutrition information (dichotomous) with disordered eating. Higher self-reported nutrition knowledge was associated with elevated odds of binge eating, preoccupation with thinness, and self-induced vomiting. Seeking nutrition information online was associated with higher odds of binge eating in Australia and Mexico; lower odds of self-induced vomiting in Australia, Canada, Mexico, and the United States; and higher odds of preoccupation with thinness in Canada, the United Kingdom, and the United States. Disordered eating is cross-sectionally associated with self-reported nutrition knowledge and seeking nutrition information from online sources. Results indicate a greater need for public health nutrition research and education.
